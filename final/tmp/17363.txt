\n\n Death Blight Status Effect \n\nEffect\nInstant Death\n\nMitigated by\nPrince of Death's PustulePrince of Death's Cyst\n\nCured by\nRejuvenating BolusesOrder HealingLaw of Regression\n\nResistive Stat\n Vitality\n\nDeath Blight is a Status Effect in Elden Ring. Status Effects are various buffs or ailments that apply either a beneficial or detrimental effect to the target. Status Effects can be typically be applied to both enemies and players alike, however Death Blight only affects other Tarnished. Status Effects are triggered after a buildup meter is filled by repeated application of attacks that cause the effect. Both players and enemies can have varying resistances to a given Status Effect.\n\nElden Ring Death Blight Guide\nDeath Blight is a status effect that builds up through repeated application of the effect. Once the meter is filled, the effect is triggered and the player dies instantly. Unlike other status effects, Death Blight is only capable of being inflicted on other Tarnished.\nVitality is the main defensive stat that determines your resistance to Death Blight.\n\nLeveling up increases your defensive stats including Vitality\nLeveling up Arcane increases your Vitality by a larger amount than any other attributes\n\nDeath Blight Effects in Elden Ring\n\nThe Death Blight effect build up can be mitigated by raising your Vitality stat.\nWhen the buildup bar is full, the target suffers Instant Death. The Blight Status Effect mostly works on PCs; other enemies are immune (with the notable exception of NPC Invaders).\n\nElden Ring Instant Death Effect on Players\nThis section lists information QUESTION
What are some ways to mitigate and cure the Death Blight status effect in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
Raising Vitality, using Prince of Death's Pustule and Cyst to mitigate, and Order Healing and Law of Regression to cure can help mitigate and cure Death Blight in Elden Ring.
