\n\n Spirit Jellyfish Ashes \n\nType\nSpirit Summon\n\nFP Cost 31\nHP Cost -\n\nEffect \n\n        Summons spirit jellyfish. \n\nSpirit Jellyfish Ashes is a Spirit and Summon in Elden Ring. Spirit Jellyfish Ashes summons a giant stationary Jellyfish that perform ranged and melee attacks to near enemies.\nThe Summoned Spirit Jellyfish, floats around and moves slowly. It can resist many attacks, but it needs a lot of time to perform an attack against enemies and this attack can be easily interrupted. It might be more strategical to use Jellyfish against bosses, not only to tank them out, if you distract the boss to let the jellyfish finish their attack, the boss will gain a damage debuff. \nSpirit Jellyfish can also be found as Enemies in Elden Ring. For more information, see Spirit Jellyfish.\n\nAshen remains in which spirits yet dwell. Use to summon a spirit jellyfish.\nA floating spirit that illuminates its surroundings. Prone to tears, the jellyfish girl searches for her distant home. Will bravely spew poison at foes on her summoner's behalf.\nIt seems her name is Aurelia.\n\nSpirit Jellyfish Ashes Location in Elden Ring\nWhere to find Spirit Jellyfish Ashes:\n\nLimgrave: In Stormhill Shack there's a woman with a red hood named Roderika that will give you x1 Spirit Jellyfish Ashes by talking to her several times. QUESTION
Why would it be more strategic to use the Jellyfish summon against bosses in Elden Ring instead of regular enemies?

ANSWER
The Jellyfish summon can distract bosses to gain a damage debuff besides tanking out, making it a more strategic choice.
