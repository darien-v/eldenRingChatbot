They are descendants of the Giants, who were mortal enemies of the Erdtree, yet in the War against the Giants, they fought for the Golden Order against their brethren. Some trolls are also loyal to the Carian royal family - these Troll knights can be found carrying the Troll Knight's Sword. The stone hammer that the Stonedigger Trolls carry were ceremonial relics used in smithing, an act that was once seen as divine.\nThis is an optional boss, as players don't need to defeat it in order to advance. These towering creatures may have a tough exterior, but they can be easily toppled; beware of their high level of Hardness, but overcome it and they are easily staggered. Sleep is also effective for this fight due to the Troll's low resistance to the status effect. They drop the Roar Medallion and Great Club when defeated.\nBosses are unique and challenging Enemies that can be encountered all over the Lands Between. Great Enemy bosses can drop unique Spirit Ashes, Weapons, Spells, and other various Items.\nTrolls can be found as a regular enemy in the Lands Between.\n\n Trolls are descended from the giants, and their hammers were supposedly once used as ceremonial smithing tools. For example, if a negation is 60, 40% of that damage by that type will go through and 60% will be negated. Bigger number = less damage. A negation of 100 means no damage goes through, and a negation of -100 mean the enemy takes 2x damage from that source. 0 means damage goes pretty much as is.\n\nStonedigger Troll Boss Guide for Elden Ring\n\nStonedigger Troll Fight Strategy\nThe Best Tips for Stonedigger Troll:\n\nUse the Kaiden Sellsword Ashes for an easier fight or Spirit Jellyfish Ashes as a distraction.\nThe Troll is extremely weak to Sleep. A single Sleep Pot will put it to sleep no matter how low your Arcane or how many times it's already slept.\n\nMelee Strategy\nThe Troll has high Hardness, so most one-handed melee attacks will rebound off it preventing you from landing chain attacks. You can prevent this by two-handing your weapon or using a weapon with higher Hardness. Also, beware of its large area-of-effect attacks and give yourself plenty of time to move out of the way or roll through them. Otherwise, the safest place to be is near his feet, and ideally behind him as well. You can still get hit by his Stomp, but follow-up moves will mostly miss you.\nStance Breaking: Since the troll has very low Stance, it doesn't take many fully-charged heavy attacks or jump attacks to get it to collapse onto the floor. Land a critical hit if you want, or just land more blows until it gets back up.\n\nMagic and Ranged Strategy\nThe Troll is slow but hits with large area-of-affect attacks, so make sure you aren't caught in them. It's best to use a shield in one hand and your staff or seal in the other, in case you need to guard in an emergency. Bait his attacks, then roll away to create separation before casting.\n\nAttacks & Counters\n\nAttack\nDescription\nCounter\n\nStomp\nRaises its leg and stomps its down. It'll continue stomping the same leg until you're out of range\nRoll towards the leg that didn't stomp and land a hit on it\n\nSwing\nSwings its hammer either along the ground or down in front of it\nRoll towards a horizontal sweep or away from a vertical one, or else stay safe near its legs and land a hit\n\nArm Sweep\nSweeps its arm along the ground\nRoll towards the arm\n\nColumn Slam\nSlams its hammer down headfirst directly into the ground in front of it, creating a shockwave\nRoll towards its legs\n\nKneeling Swing\nKneels on the ground and braces itself with its hand and swings its hammer in a wide counterclockwise arc with extra reach\nRoll towards the hammer\n\nMega Slam\nGrabs its hammer with both hands and holds it above its head higher and longer than for a normal vertical swing, then slams it into the ground releasing a huge shockwave\nThe safest strategy is to run away, but you can also get in a hit while it's winding up and then jump over the shockwave\n\nOverhead Combo\nLets out a furious growl and slams its hammer into the ground in front of it four times. Next, raises its left leg for a few seconds before doing one more colossal hammer slam. Finally, pulls its hammer out of the ground with a shockwave. Only does this below 60% HP\nThis attack is strong, but it doesn't have very good tracking. Stay near its legs and you'll be fine except for the final slam, which has a wide enough shockwave that you should try to roll it if you're anywhere nearby\n\nRoar\nRoars, creating a large shockwave and increasing the Troll’s damage by 20% for 15 seconds\nMove far away or roll through the shockwave\n\nElden Ring Stonedigger Troll Lore, Notes & Other Trivia\n\nThe Stonedigger Trolls wield the Troll's Hammer, a ceremonial smithing tool, suggesting that they used to be blacksmiths. In the distant past, smithing was considered divine.
