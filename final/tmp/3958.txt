Grafted Scion is an amorphous mass of bodies that wields two swords and a greatshield, found in various locales, most prominently in Stormveil Castle. This is an optional boss as players don't need to defeat it to advance in the game.\n\nElden Ring Grafted Scion Location\n\nChapel of Anticipation [More Info ↓] [Map Link]\n2 in Fringefolk Hero's Grave [More Info ↓]\nStormveil Castle [More Info ↓] [Map Link]\nLiurnia of the Lakes (disguised as a Giant Crayfish) [More Info ↓]\nMt. For example, if a negation is 60, 40% of that damage by that type will go through and 60% will be negated. Bigger number = less damage. A negation of 100 means no damage goes through, and a negation of -100 mean the enemy takes 2x damage from that source. 0 means damage goes pretty much as is.\n\nElden Ring Grafted Scion Boss Guide\n\n Grafted Scion Fight Strategy\nThe Best Tips for Grafted Scion:\n\nAvoid guarding as much as possible, focus on rolling instead.\nUse quick to cast spells like Glintstone Pebble.\nVagabound and Samurai classes are the best to kill him during 1st encounter due to their stance-breaking skills. \n\nMelee Strategy\nBait: Keep your distance and try to get it to use Leaping Stab, Crawling Stabs or (later in the fight) Piercing Stabs. Dodge the attack, get in a couple hits, and then back off. Don't get greedy: it will often use Scream after being damaged, and you don't want to be nearby for that. You can also watch for two consecutive clockwise slashes: this always signals the end of a combo, and you can safely get in a hit after it as well.\nMagic and Ranged Strategy\nBide Your Time: Keep your distance and focus on dodging if you can and guarding if you have to. Don't attack until you know it's safe, such as during the long recovery period after Leaping Stab, Crawling Stabs, its double-sword Stab, or (later in the fight) Piercing Stabs. It has long combos and can react to being shot with moves that will close distance, so stay on your toes once it has recovered.\n\nGrafted Scion Attacks & Counters\n\nAttack\nDescription\nCounter\n\nSlash\nSlashes one or both of its swords in various directions\nUsually used as part of a combo, so dodge away and wait for the combo to finish\n\nRolling Slash\nSwings both its swords in one direction while rolling, then swings them twice again before scuttling back a bit\nDodge away twice.\n\nStab\nQuickly stabs one or both of its swords forward\nDodge to either side\n\nLeaping Stab\nLeaps high into the air, then stabs its sword straight down into the ground\nRoll forward a beat after it reaches the apex of its leap as it raises its shield, then get in an attack while it recovers\n\nShield Slam\nLifts its shield high above its head, then slams it down\nRoll clockwise around it as its body begins to descend and get in an attack while it recovers\n\nShield Bash\nShoves its shield forward\nRoll back\n\nCrawling Stabs\nStands up higher and stabs the ground while walking forward, then leaps and slams down all its weapons\nWalk around it while it moves, then roll forward when its body begins to fall and get in a hit while it recovers\n\nRetreat\nStabs one sword then slashes clockwise with the other while scuttling backwards\nDodge back and to the side\n\nScream\nLowers its head and screams, dealing damage to anything closeby.\nRoll back and keep your distance until it's done. Guarding isn't effective against this attack. If you're quick, you can get in one hit between this attack ending and it starting another combo\n\nGolden Tempering\nCrosses its swords together, buffing them with Holy damage for one minute. Only used below 50% health, and only in the Chapel of Anticipation and Mt. Gelmir\nThis attack takes almost three full seconds and doesn't do any damage, so use it as an opportunity to get in a few hits\n\nFencing Stance\nStands up tall and holds one sword above it in a fencing stance, preparing to do a powerful attack. Only used below 60% health\nTry to stay far away to encourage it to use Piercing Stabs, which is easier to punish\n\nSpinning Slash\nRaises up its lower sword, then spins clockwise four and a half times with swords extended. It will push its shield away from its body when the attack is about to end. Used in Fencing Stance you're nearby\nRun away when it raises up its lower sword and circle behind it as it spins, then move in as the attack finishes to get in a hit while it recovers\n\nPiercing Stabs\nSpreads its swords wide, then stabs them forward three times, pauses briefly, stabs seven more times, pauses again, then stabs both swords across one another in front of it. Used in Fencing Stance when you're further away\nMove behind it when you see it spread its swords and start attacking as soon as you're not at risk of getting hit\n\n: Attack can be parried\nGrafted Scion Lore, Notes & Other Trivia\nFAQ: How do you kill the Spider Elden Ring?\n\nMost player's first encounter with this Spider Boss known as the Grafted Scion will not usually end in its defeat especially since this occurs at the very beginning of the game. This encounter is part of the tutorial process and although you must face this Boss to progress, it is not required that you defeat it at this time. In fact, it is not expected that you have a successful initial encounter since this area can be returned to at a later time in the game which some players may not find out until later on. Complete this encounter with either a win or defeat and continue about your game until you reach The Four Belfries. \nIf you didn't defeat Grafted Scion during the tutorial, you can return and face it again through the waygate marked "Precipice of Anticipation" in The Four Belfries. This can be done after you have returned with more experience, improve your equipment and level. This is especially beneficial for people who want to receive its unique drops: Ornamental Straight Sword and Golden Beast Crest Shield. Players can also face the same type of Spider boss at the  Fringefolk Hero's Grave, Stormveil Castle and Liurnia of the Lakes. See the Location guides above on how to recognize the Boss arena and how to get there. \nUse the strategy guides and keep your distance against this Boss while wearing out its hp eventually leading to its defeat. Use a combination of dodging and waiting for attack windows before closing in for your attack. Once you have the opportunity to approach, close in and deal a few attacks before distancing your character again and repeating the same process. \n\nGrafted Scion Other Notes\n\nDuring the Network Test, many players have previously called this nameless mini-boss the Many Armed Key Master\nThis enemy is likely the “Spider” that Roderika speaks about\n\nGrafted Scion Combo Chart\n\n Grafted Scion  Chapel of Anticipation  \n\nDrops\n3,200Ornamental Straight SwordGolden Beast Crest Shield\n\nHP\n2,596\n\nElden Ring Grafted Scion Boss (Chapel of Anticipation)\n\nThis is not an optional boss, but unlike other mandatory bosses does not have to be defeated to progress.\nClosest Site of Grace: The Four Belfries\nMultiplayer is not allowed for this boss\nYou cannot summon  Spirit Ashes  for this boss\n\nGrafted Scion Location\nGrafted Scion is on the island at the end of the tutorial section at the beginning of the game. QUESTION
What are the best strategies for defeating the Grafted Scion boss in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
Avoid guarding as much as possible, focus on rolling instead, use quick to cast spells like Glintstone Pebble, Vagabound and Samurai classes are best due to stance-breaking skills. Bait and observe attack patterns for melee, bide your time for magic and ranged, and utilize the weaknesses and counters provided in the fight guide.
