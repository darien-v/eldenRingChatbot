CONTEXT
\n\n Sword Dance \n\n        Quickly close in to perform a series of spinning upward slashes. Follow up with an additional input to finish with a downward slash. \n\nSword Dance is a Skill in Elden Ring. Sword Dance is a regular skill that can be found in Ashes of War and applied to compatible weapons. Updated to Patch 1.07.\n\nHow to get Sword Dance\n\nAsh of War: Sword Dance - Dropped by a Teardrop Scarab along the path leading to the Road's End Catacombs below the Minor Erdtree in southwest Liurnia. Elden Ring Map Link.\n\nElden Ring Sword Dance Guide, Notes & Tips\n\nThis is an Ash of War Skill, associated with the Ash of War: Sword Dance.\nFP Cost: 6 on use per input. \n\nThis totals to a 12 FP cost.\n\nStance damage: 8+8+9, total 27 \nThis Skill is not Chargeable\nAll 3 separate slashes can be Parried\nUpon activation, the user will spin forward a moderate distance and hit twice. \n\nAn aditional input from the Skill button will slam the armament to the ground, hitting again.\n\nDistance covered is the same, regardless of weapon class.\nThe speed of both inputs in relation to the attacks can vary. This is based on the weapon class of the armament in question that has the skill.\n\n"Small" armaments such as Daggers, Straight Swords, Curved Swords, Katanas, and Axes have overall fast speed on both inputs. There's almost no delay between the input and the attacks.\n"Large" armaments such as Greatswords, Twinblades, Greataxes, Spears, Halberds, and Scythes (Reapers) have a slight attack delay on the first input, and a longer, considerable delay on the second input.\n\nAll instances of this Weapon Skill can be parried.\nIf used without FP: \n\nReceives a damage penalty\nReduces Stance damage dealt.\n\nNote: This Skill was changed with Patch 1.07 as follows: The delay between the end of various actions was reduced (Actions such as using items or attack animations) and being able to perform the skill.\nTrivia\n\nThe attack speed based on the type of weapon is a behavior shared with the Weapon Skill, Double Slash.\nThis Weapon Skill appeared first in Dark Souls 3, as a exclusive Weapon Art for the Follower Sabre. \n\nThe name of the Weapon Art was Prying Wedge, and has exactly the same animation.\n\nSword Dance Demonstration\n\nBuilds with Sword Dance\n\nBlackflame Spellblade
