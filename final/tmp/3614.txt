\n\n Rosus's Summons \n\n        Raise the axe aloft to summon those lost in death. Three skeletons will appear at a distance and attack in tandem before vanishing \n\nRosus's Summons is a Skill in Elden Ring. Rosus's Summons is a Unique skill found on the Rosus' Axe. "Raise the axe aloft to summon those lost in death. Three skeletons will appear at a distance and attack in tandem before vanishing." . Updated to Patch 1.07.\n\nHow to get Rosus's Summons\n\nDeafult skill for the Rosus' Axe.\n\nElden Ring Rosus's Summons Guide\n\nThis is a Unique Skill\nFP Cost: 15\nDeals 30 stance damage when all attacks hit.\nDeals 810 PvP poise damage when all attacks hit. \n\nAs they all have the same poise damage if you want to calculate what X number of hits will do divide by 4 then multiply it by the number of attacks that are hitting. \n\nDeals both Physical and Magic Damage\nThe motion values for Rosus's Summons - Bullet (Skeletons) are 105 Physical and Magic\nRosus's Summons - Weapon hit has a motion value of 100 Physical and Magic \nThis Skill has Hyperarmor, it has a poise multiplier of 2 (it actually reduces poise damage taken by .5 but for all intents and purposes it multiplies your poise by 2) and gets 30 poise from the axe itself. \n\nThis Skill is not Chargeable\nThis Skill cannot be Parried\n\nThis Skill can be Parried if performed with no Focus\n\nNote: This Skill was changed with Patch 1.07 as follows: motion speed has been increased.\n\nElden Ring Skills Demonstration\n\nElden Ring Rosus's Summons Notes & Tips\n\nUpdated to patch 1.07. QUESTION
Why is Rosus's Summons a unique skill in Elden Ring and how does it differ from other skills in the game?

ANSWER
Rosus's Summons is a unique skill in Elden Ring because it involves summoning three skeletons to attack together, has hyperarmor, deals both physical and magic damage, cannot be charged, cannot be parried during focus, and was updated in Patch 1.07 to increase motion speed.
