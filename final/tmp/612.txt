CONTEXT
\n\n Lunar Estate Ruins \n\nThe ruins of a large estate found atop the southwestern plateau of Liurnia.\n\nLunar Estate Ruins is a Location in Elden Ring. The Lunar Estate Ruins is found at the Moonlight Altar plateau, high above in the southwest Liurnia of the Lakes. The Moonlight Altar is accessible only after making significant progress in Ranni's questline, up to the point where you defeat Astel, Naturalborn of the Void in the Grand Cloister and gain access to the lift in the Deep Ainsel Well, which leads up to the plateau. Players can reach the Lunar Estate Ruins by traveling east from the Moonlight Altar site of grace. \n\nLunar Estate Ruins Map\nThe Lunar Estate Ruins are located on the top of the plateau of the southern landmass in Liurnia. There is no way to access the top of the plateau until you have progressed Ranni's questline. Beware, because a Revenant spawns in an adjacent room. The chest below contains Smithing Stone (8) x3.\nHead towards the central section of the ruins and you will face another Revenant. Search the ruined buildings for a corpse containing a Golden Rune (10). There is another corpse nearby where you can loot Glintstone Firefly x 5. Three Wraith Callers will spawn in as you approach the body.\nFinally, check the buildings north from where you are and look for an Imp Statue by a wall. To its right is an illusory floor leading to another cellar. Dispel the floor and then insert a Stonesword Key into the statue to unlock the seal. Another Wraith Caller will spawn as you do. QUESTION
Why is progress in Ranni's questline necessary to access the Lunar Estate Ruins in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
Progress in Ranni's questline is necessary to access the Lunar Estate Ruins in Elden Ring because it unlocks the Moonlight Altar plateau, providing access to the lift in the Deep Ainsel Well that leads up to the plateau.
