CONTEXT
\n\n Old Lord's Talisman 古き王のタリスマン \n\nWeight\n0.5\n\nEffect \n\n Extends the duration of  \n       Spells by 30% \n\nOld Lord's Talisman is a Talisman in Elden Ring. Old Lord's Talisman extends the duration of Spells by 30%. Players can use talismans in Elden Ring to boost a variety of Stats.\n\nA legendary talisman depicting the ancient king whose seat lies at the heart of the storm beyond time.\nExtends the duration of sorceries and incantations.\nIt is said that the ancient royal city of Farum Azula has been slowly crumbling since time immemorial.\n\nOld Lord's Talisman Effect in Elden Rin\nOld Lord's Talisman extends the duration of Spells by 30%.\n\nWhere to find Old Lord's Talisman in Elden Ring\nFound in a chest in Crumbling Farum Azula. [Map link]\nThe chest is inside a rotunda on the east edge of the Dragon Temple Rooftop area. This area is accessed by heading north from the Great Bridge and entering the building there. Exit onto the balcony on the right, and take the ladder nearby down to the walkway leading to the rotunda. Beware as the chest is surrounded by Beastmen and, if you've killed Rykard in the Volcano Manor, you will also be invaded by Recusant Bernahl on the walkway.\nVideo Location\n\nElden Ring Old Lord's Talisman Notes & Tips\n\nSell Value:  1,000\nAffects both Sorceries and Incantations.\nAffects HP regeneration incantations, such as Blessing of the Erdtree and Bestial Vitality. \n\nThis boosts their total healing and duration by 30%.\n\nSpells and buffs cast by other players that apply their effects on the talisman's user are affected, and have their duration extended. \n\nThis is regardless of the player casting the spell having the talisman equipped or not.\n\nDoes not affect spells or buffs created by Ashes of War, except for Rallying Standard.\nDoes not increase the duration of buffs applied to Spirit Ashes (tested on 1.04).\nDoes not increase the distance a projectile sorcery travels (e.g. Glintstone Pebble).\nThe extended duration does not work if the player removes the talisman (i.e. cast buff, then remove talisman).\nThe duration is not affected much if the player equips the talisman near the end of the buff. \n\nTesting results shows that the talisman adds spell duration based on the amount of time the talisman has been equipped, in relation to the length of the spell. This is always a 30% duration increase in relation to that time.\nAn example of this would be: Having the talisman equipped for a total of  10 seconds would increase the spell length by 3 seconds. Having it for 7 seconds will increase the length by 2.1 seconds (rounded down), and so forth. Always having the talisman equipped will give the full benefit of the extended spell duration.\n\nOld Lord's Talisman Builds\n\nBlack Arrow Written Guide\n\nTrivia\n\nThe effect of this talisman affects positively heal-over-time effects. This is unlike its equivalent predecessor in the Souls franchise, the Lingering Dragoncrest Ring, where only duration (but not total amount of HP healed) was affected.\n\nElden Ring
