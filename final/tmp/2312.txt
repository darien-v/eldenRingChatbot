\n\n Soap \n\nEffect \n\n        Removes filth and accumulations on the body \n\nScaling \n\n        - \n\nFP Cost\n-\n\nSoap is a Consumable Item in Elden Ring. Most Consumables grant various effects such as restorative properties for recovering HP, removing certain negative status effects or applying temporary buffs and debuffs, while others can be thrown at enemies to deal damage.\n\nA plant-based soap made from mushroom juice.Craftable item.\nCleans off filth and other accumulations on the body while also slightly reducing poison buildup.\nFilth covering the exterior eventually seeps inside, soiling one's very spirit.\n\nWhere to find Soap in Elden Ring\n\nCan be purchased from the Nomadic Merchant in Siofra River for 400 Runes. [Map Link] Video Location\nFour can be looted from a corpse guarded by a basilisk in the ant nest near Ainsel River Downstream. [Map Link]\nAinsel River Video Location\n\nSoap use in Elden Ring\nSoap can be used to remove filth and other accumulations on the player's body while also slightly reducing poison buildup.\n\nElden Ring Soap Crafting Guide\nTo craft Soap you need the Nomadic Warrior's Cookbook [17] as well as the following Crafting Materials:\n\nMelted Mushroom x2\n\nElden Ring Soap Notes & Tips\n\nYou can hold up to 99 Soap.\nYou can store up to 600 Soap.\nSell Value:  10 Runes\nHas cosmetic uses, to keep your character looking pristine.\nIt's easy to craft in large quantities, making it easy to spam when traveling through poisonous areas to reduce overreliance on other curative methods.\nAccumulations on the body aren't just cosmetic. Some examples are as follows \n\nBeing wet reduces your lightning resistance and increases your fire resistance slightly.\nBeing covered in oil causes the next fire damage you receive to deal significantly more damage to you.\nBeing covered in poisonous swamp water will cause the water's inflicted ailment to build up for a time, even after you leave said swamp.\n\nElden Ring
