These towering humanoids wield massive weapons and wear distinctive pumpkin-shaped helmets. These are optional bosses as players are not required to defeat them to advance in Elden Ring.\nLesser Mad Pumpkin Head is the normal enemy form of this boss. Mad Pumpkin Heads can be summoned as spirits for aid in battle through Mad Pumpkin Head Ashes.\n\nThough he has stifled his panic within the dark confines of his helmet, he rampages as if driven mad when agitated by bloodshed or the humming of insects. This mad soldier is all that is left of a broken gladiator. \n\nElden Ring Mad Pumpkin Head Location\nMad Pumpkin Head can be found in:\n\nWaypoint Ruins [More Info] [Map Link]\nCaelem Ruins [More Info] [Map Link]\n\nMad Pumpkin Head Combat information\n\nStance: 65\nParriable: No\nIs vulnerable to a critical hit after being stance broken or parried\nThe head has extra armor, and attacks that hit it will do only 20% of base damage.\n\nNegations (or Absorptions)\n\nStandard: 0\nSlash: -10\nStrike: 10\nPierce: 0\n\nMagic: 0\nFire: 0\nLightning: -20\nHoly: 0\n\nThe negation numbers are the % of your damage that gets blocked. For example, if a negation is 60, 40% of that damage by that type will go through and 60% will be negated. Bigger number = less damage. A negation of 100 means no damage goes through, and a negation of -100 mean the enemy takes 2x damage from that source. 0 means damage goes pretty much as is.\n\nElden Ring Mad Pumpkin Head Boss Guide\n\nMad Pumpkin Head Fight Strategy\nThe Best Tips for Mad Pumpkin Head:\n\nSummon the Lone Wolf Ashes to help stagger him, giving you an opening for free damage.\nAttack from behind to avoid hitting his head.\n\nMelee Users\nAfter a sequence of slow but heavy attacks, he will slowly reposition himself before attacking again. This is your chance to punish him. When dodging his attacks, try to end up behind him so you can hit his unprotected back once he's finished.\nMagic and Ranged Users\nAvoid shooting his head with spells or arrows. You can use Spirit Ashes to distract him so you can hit his back, or keep moving and rolling away to create enough distance to fire off spells or arrows.\n\nMad Pumpkin Head Attacks & Counters\n\nAttack\nDescription\nCounter\n\nHand Strike\nStrikes with his weaponless left hand \nSidestep or roll backwards\n\nHead Slam\nRears his pumpkin head back, then slams it down in front of him\nRoll forward through the head slam, then attack his back while he recovers\n\nFlail Variant\n\nDouble Swing\nSwings his flail twice slowly, first counterclockwise then clockwise\nMove backwards or roll to the side\n\nGround Hit\nLeans back, then thrusts the flail violently into the ground\nRoll to either side\n\nUp-Down Combo\nSwings the flail up and then immediately back down\nRoll to either side\n\nBig Swing\nHolds or twirls the flail behind it, then swings counterclockwise\nRoll to the right\n\nMad Pumpkin Head (Flail) Combo Chart\n\n Mad Pumpkin Head Waypoint Ruins \n\nDrops\n1,100\n\nHP\n1,328\n\nElden Ring Mad Pumpkin Head Boss (Waypoint Ruins)\n\nOptional boss\nClosest Site of Grace: Agheel Lake South\nMultiplayer allowed for this boss\nYou can summon Spirit Ashes for this boss\n\nMad Pumpkin Head Location\nUnderneath Waypoint Ruins. QUESTION
What are the key strategies for defeating Mad Pumpkin Head in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
Summon Lone Wolf Ashes for staggering, attack from behind, use Spirit Ashes for distraction, avoid hitting the head with spells or arrows, end up behind him after dodging attacks, and capitalize on his slow repisitioning for punishment.
