Updated to Patch 1.07.\n\nOne of the glintstone sorceries of the Academy of Raya Lucaria.\nFires twin projectiles that form a spiral as they travel. This sorcery can be cast repeatedly and while in motion.Charging enhances potency.\nA sorcery of the Karolos Conspectus, the most venerable of the academy. This was the product of a failed attempt to create a new comet.\n\nWhere to find Shard Spiral\nWhere to find Shard Spiral :\n\nAvailable for purchase from the Sorcery Instruction of Sorceress Sellen for 8000 after progressing through her questline and choosing to side with her.\n\nElden Ring Shard Spiral Guide\n\nGlintstone Sorcery\nStamina Cost: 28\nDeals magic damage\nThe primary draw of this sorcery is its use against larger targets. When cast, the spiraling pair of magic bolts will slowly fly toward their target and then continue to fly through them instead of exploding on contact. The magic bolts will continue to deal damage as they fly through the target until they reach their maximum range, at which point the bolts expire. As such, this sorcery can do considerably more damage than many other sorceries, provided the magic bolts are allowed to travel through their target as long as possible.\nFaster cast time (especially when uncharged) and lower FP cost than other high damage sorceries, more equivalent to Great Glintstone Shard in terms of speed and cost \n\nThe drawback is its slow travel time and moderate damage against smaller enemies\n\nNOTE: This Glintstone Sorcery was changed with Patch 1.07 as follows: The Attack Power was Increased.\n\nElden Ring Shard Spiral Notes and tips \n\nUpdated to patch 1.07. QUESTION
Why is Shard Spiral considered a more effective sorcery against larger targets compared to smaller enemies?

ANSWER
Shard Spiral is more effective against larger targets due to the ability of the magic bolts to continue dealing damage as they pass through the target until reaching their maximum range, resulting in considerably more damage compared to smaller enemies.
