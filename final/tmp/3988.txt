\n\n Frostbite Status Effect \n\nEffect\nDeals damage and lowers target's damage absorption by 20%\n\nMitigated by\nInvigorating Cured MeatStalwart Horn Charm\n\nCured by\nBestial ConstitutionThawfrost Boluses\n\nResistive Stat\nRobustness\n\nFrostbite is a Status Effect in Elden Ring. Status Effects are various buffs or ailments that apply either a beneficial or detrimental effect to the target. Status Effects can be applied on both enemies and players alike. Status Effects are triggered after a buildup meter is filled by repeated application of attacks that cause the effect. Both players and enemies can have varying resistances to a given Status Effect.\n\nElden Ring Frostbite Guide\nFrostbite is a status effect that builds up through repeated application of the effect. Frostbite is inflicted via various means including Weapons, Skills, and Spells. Once the Frostbite meter is filled, the effect is triggered, dealing damage, and for the 30-second duration, increasing damage taken by 20%.\nFrostbite is effective against most enemies that still have flesh. \n\nFrostbite Effects in Elden Ring\nFrostbite deals an instance of damage upon triggering, and then lowers the target's damage absorption by 20%.\n\nFrostbite inflicts 10% of a normal enemy's max HP plus 30 damage.\nBosses, as well as normal enemies located in boss rooms, will take 7% of their max HP, plus 30 damage.\nDebuff increases all damage taken by 20% for 30 seconds.\nDebuff can be removed by inflicting fire damage.\nFrostbite cannot begin building again until the debuff is gone, either through timing out or removal by fire.\nFrostbite buildup can be mitigated by raising your Robustness stat. Robustness can be improved by adding points into Endurance.\n\nFrostbite Sources (Weapons)\nThe following weapons cause the Frostbite effect:\n\nA Cold affinity on a weapon via use of Ashes of QUESTION
Why is fire damage needed to remove the Frostbite debuff?

ANSWER
Fire damage is needed to remove the Frostbite debuff because it effectively cancels out the Frostbite effect, allowing for a quick reapplication of Frostbite.
