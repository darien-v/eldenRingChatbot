\n\n Omen \n\nLocation\nStormveil CastleCliffbottom CatacombsUnsightly CatacombsAltus PlateauAuriza Hero's GraveLeyndell CatacombsSubterranean Shunning-Grounds\n\nDrops\n 451 - 2520 RunesOmen CleaverWarped Axe\n\nHP\n1155 - 6539\n\nOmen is a type of Monster Enemy in Elden Ring. Omen are curse-born, ogre-like beings that favor using large and heavy weapons such as Greatswords or Greataxes. They are normally slow moving and can often be found sleeping or otherwise distracted. Omen will, however, move surprisingly fast and erupt with great ferocity when provoked, jumping into battle with multiple wide swings and attacks.\nOmen born to commoners often have their horns excised (cut off), generally ending in the child's death, while Omen born to Nobility are allowed to retain their gnarled horns, but are hidden away underground to live in obscurity.\n\nElden Ring Omen Locations\nWhere to Find Omen:\n\nStormveil Castle\n\nYou can find the Omen at the heavily guarded courtyard of the castle. The nearest checkpoint is the Liftside Chamber Site of Grace.\n\nCliffbottom Catacombs\nAltus Plateau\nThere is one near the end of Unsightly Catacombs.\nMinor Erdtree (Capital Outskirts) guarding Twiggy Cracked Tear and Crimson Crystal Tear. This Omen can use Fia's Mist, just like the Commoners accompanying it. The Commoners also all wear a small face shroud, suggesting a relationship to Deathbed Companions, or perhaps Fia herself.\nAuriza Hero's Grave\nSubterranean Shunning-Grounds\nCapital Outskirts: Two Omens appear as the field boss Fell Twins.\n\nElden Ring Omen Combat information\n\nHealth: 1155 - 6539 HP\nPoise: 65\nDeals Slash Damage\n\nAbsorptions\n\nPhy (Standard): 0\nPhy (Slash): -11\nPhy (Strike): 0\nPhy (Pierce): 0\n\nMagic: 0\nFire: 0\nLightning: 0\nHoly: 20 - 47\n\nThe absorption numbers are the % of your damage that gets blocked. For example if an absorption is 60, 40% of that damage by that type will go through and 60% is absorbed. Bigger number = less damage. An absorption of 100 means no damage goes through, and a resistance of -100 mean the enemy takes 2x damage from that source. 0 means damage goes pretty much as is.\n\nResistances\n\nPoison: 227 - 250\nScarlet Rot: 227 - 250\nHemorrhage: 227 - 250\nFrostbite: 227 - 250\n\nThe resistance numbers are the buildup amount to trigger it. For example, if a resistance is 100 you must deal 100 points of whatever aux buildup to trigger it. Note that these go down over time, and increase each time the effect procs.\n\nElden Ring Omen Drops and Drop Rates\n  Runes: 1111 - 2520.\n\n Item \n Drop Rate \n Additional Info \n\n Runes \n100.00%\n  451 - 2520 \n\n Omen Cleaver \n4.00%\n Dropped only by those that wield it. \n\n Warped Axe \n4.00%\n Dropped only by those that wield it. \n\n Omen Bairn \n100%\n Dropped only by one Omen in Subterranean Shunning-Grounds \n\nOmen Notes & Tips\n\nOne of the few enemies that are attracted to Shadow Bait.\nSleeping Omen can sometimes be found in the Altus Plateau. They appear to be suffering from nightmares, convulsing and writhing around in their sleep.\nThere are two variants, those with cut horns, and those with uncut horns, and each variant can carry either an Omen Cleaver or a Warped Axe. The uncut horn variant can summon wraiths and have some moves that have black-and-gold cursed flames, like the Revenants and Wraith Callers.\nOmenkillers may have been created to cull the Omen population. The Omensmirk Mask are said to be in imitation of the evil spirits in the Omen’s nightmares.\nA shield with the Barricade Shield skill will cause the Omen to recoil when hitting the shield, allowing a QUESTION
How can players effectively counter an Omen's attacks in combat in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
Using a shield with the Barricade Shield skill will cause the Omen to recoil, allowing for a risk-free guard counter.
