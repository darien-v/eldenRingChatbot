\n\n Moonfolk Ruins \n\nRuins found in the southwest of the Liurnia of the Lakes region\n\nMoonfolk Ruins is a Location in Elden Ring. The Moonfolk Ruins is found in Liurnia of the Lakes region. The ruins can be found up the hill from the Moonlight Altar Site of Grace on the way to the Cathedral of Manus Celes. This location is accessible only after you have made significant progress in Ranni's questline.\n\nMoonfolk Ruins Map\nThe Moonfolk ruins can be found on our interactive map [Elden Ring Map here].\n\nAll NPCs and Merchants in Moonfolk Ruins\n\nNone found here.\n\nAll Items in Elden Ring's Moonfolk Ruins\n\nGather & Farm Items\n\nMushroom\nHerba\nRowa Fruit\n\nUpgrade Materials\n\nSomber Smithing Stone (8)\n\nEquipment and Magic\n\nThere are no equipment in this location\n\nUnique, Ashes and Keys\n\nThere are no key items in this location\n\nElden Ring Moonfolk Ruins Creatures, Enemies, and Bosses\n\nRegular Creatures and Enemies\n\nDeer\nNoble Sorcerer\n\nField Bosses and Bosses\n\nThere are no bosses in this location\n\nMoonfolk Ruins Walkthrough\nThere is little of note in these ruins other than an illusory floor in the northeast section of the ruins where a path to an underground treasure room can be found.\n\nHit the floor to reveal the path and collect a Somber Smithing Stone (8) from the chest in the room. Beware when traversing the ruins as there are plenty of teleport-capable Noble Sorcerers as well as glintstone traps on the ground that burst into projectiles that shoot upwards upon stepping on QUESTION
What is the significance of the illusory floor in the northeast section of Moonfolk Ruins in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
The illusory floor in the northeast section of Moonfolk Ruins in Elden Ring leads to an underground treasure room where a Somber Smithing Stone (8) can be found. Beware of teleport-capable Noble Sorcerers and glintstone traps in the ruins.
