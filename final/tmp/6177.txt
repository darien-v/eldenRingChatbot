\n\n Lion's Claw \n\n        Skill of the Redmanes, who fought alongside the General Radahn. \n       Somersault forwards, striking foes with armament.  \n\nLion's Claw is a Skill in Elden Ring. Lion's Claw is a skill found on the Claymore which allows players to somersault towards enemies while dealing damage.\n\nHow to get Lion's Claw\n\nDefault skill on the Claymore\nAsh of War: Lion's Claw - Drops from the Lion Guardian in Fort Gael upon defeating it. Elden Ring Map Link.\n\nElden Ring Lion's Claw Guide, Notes & Tips\n\nThis is an Ash of War Skill, associated with the Ash of War: Lion's Claw. \nFP Cost: 20 \nCan be used on Swords, Axes, and Hammers (small armaments and Thrusting Swords excepted).\nThis Skill not Chargeable.\nThis Skill cannot be Parried\nStance damage is dependent on weapon: \n\n30 (w/ Straight Swords, Curved Swords, Katanas, and Axes)\n33 (w/ Greatswords, Curved Greatswords, and Greataxes)\n36 (w/ Flails, Colossal Weapons (non-Hammers), and Colossal Swords)\n39 (w/ Hammers)\n42 (w/ Great Hammers) \n\n2x Lion's Claw used in rapid succession with a Great Hammer is sufficient to stagger bosses with 80 stance or below.\n\n45 (w/ Colossal Weapons (Hammers))\n\nThe initial startup where you crouch and prepare to leap into the air seems to grant you super armor which is not interruptable aside from special attacks like grabs.\nPhysical damage portion dealt it is based on the main damage type of the weapon, e.g. on a greatsword it would deal standard damage while on a morningstar it would deal strike damage.\n\nLion's Claw Demonstration\n\nElden Ring Skills\n\nAncient Lightning Spear  ♦  Angel's
