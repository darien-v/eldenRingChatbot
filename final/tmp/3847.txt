CONTEXT
\n\n Double Slash \n\n        Skill of superior swordsmen. Perform a crossing slash attack from a low stance. \n       Repeated inputs allow for up to two follow up attacks. \n\nDouble Slash is a Skill in Elden Ring. Double Slash is a regular skill that can be applied with Ashes of War to swords and polearms capable of slashing (colossal weapons excepted).\n\nHow to get Double Slash in Elden Ring\n\nAsh of War: Double Slash - Found in Sellia, Town of Sorcery: Dropped by a Teardrop Scarab found high above on a very large root in the western end of town. Accessible by jumping across the rooftops. [Elden Ring Map Link]\nDefault skill for the Serpentbone Blade.\n\nElden Ring Double Slash Guide, Notes & Tips\n\nThis is an Ash of War Skill, associated with the Ash of War: Double Slash.\nFP Cost: 6 on use. \n\n3 FP for the next two following inputs.\nUsing all the inputs totals to a 12 FP cost (6 + 3 + 3).\n\nDeals 9+12+10 ( Total 31) stance damage\nThis Skill is not Chargeable\nAll 6 separate slashes can be Parried\nUpon activation, the user briefly holds the armament (weapon) to their right side, before slashing twice side-to-side in front of them. \n\nInputting the Skill button again allows for another follow up, with the user making a brief spin before slashing thrice side-to-side.\nInputting the Skill button after this allows for one last follow up, where the user makes an overhead slash all the way to the ground.\nThe total amount of skill inputs are 3, for a total of 6 hits.\n\nEach hit consumes stamina separately, has different damage modifiers, and on-hit status buildup penalties (if weapon has any).\n\nStatus buildup is less per hit, compared to light attacks. This applies to both innate status buildup and buildup obtained from weapon buffs (such as greases).\n\nThe speed of the Weapon Skill can vary. This is based on the type of armament in question that has the skill.\n\n"Small" armaments such as Daggers, Straight Swords, Curved Swords and Katanas have overall fast speed on all attacks. There's almost no delay between each attack performed.\n"Large" armaments such as Greatswords, Twinblades, Halberds, and Scythes (Reapers) have a slight attack delay on the first input, and a longer, considerable delay on the follow up. \n\nThe recovery time of the first two inputs is the same as small armaments, the difference lies on the actual speed when performed.\n\nThe last follow up seems to have the same speed, regardless of armament. However, large armaments have a slightly longer recovery period before they can move again.\n\nStamina consumption depends on the type of armament that has the skill. Large armaments consume more stamina.\n\nWhen used without FP:\n\nHas a severe damage penalty (approximately 40% less damage compared to light attacks)\nUser can bounce off walls on hit. \n\nThis strangely enough doesn't apply to shields on PvP. Blocked attacks from Double Slash won't bounce off even without FP. However, normal enemies can make the user bounce off when blocking.\n\nGeneral Usage Tips\n\nThe weapon skill, when used on its entirety, consumes high amounts of stamina. Make sure to manage your stamina well.\nSince the weapon skill depends of player input, this allows them to use the skill, and change their approach according to the situation. \n\nFor example, using Double slash once, then dodge an incoming attack by rolling.\n\nBecause each hit uses a portion of your total damage, using small armaments with high damage is a good way to optimize this skill. \n\nThis becomes specially true when using weapon buffs, as they add damage and have a lesser damage penalty when using the skill.\n\nThis skill allows for flexible combos, as it can be used after recovery windows are finished. \n\nFor example: light attack, Double Slash, light attack. Or light attack, Double Slash, Double Slash, etc.\n\nThe reach and area coverage of every hit depends entirely of the specific weapon used. Longer weapons are more effective for this purpose.\n\nTrivia\n\nThe attack speed based on the type of weapon is a behavior shared with the Weapon Skill, Sword Dance.\nThe first two portions of this Weapon Skill look similar to the first two parts of Floating Passage, a Combat Art from the game Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice.\nCorpse Piler, a weapon skill unique to the Rivers of Blood, has an almost identical moveset. However, Corpse Piler has a trail of blood per each slash and the blood trail can hit through walls and pillars.\nThis weapon skill can be used as a low FP alternative of Corpse Piler by applying it
