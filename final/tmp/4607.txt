CONTEXT
\n\n Ash of War: Earthshaker \n\nAffinity\nHeavy\n\nSkill\nEarthshaker\n\nEffect\nGrants affinities and skills to an armament.\n\nAsh of War: Earthshaker is an Ash of War and Upgrade Material in Elden Ring. Ash of War: Earthshaker provides Heavy affinity and the Earthshaker Skill. Ashes of War can be equipped on Weapons and Shields to modify the Skill or an equipment, or to apply affinities that modify scaling values.\n\nThis Ash of War grants an armament the Heavy affinity and the following skill:\n"Earthshaker: Thrust armament into the ground, then gather strength to unleash an earth-shaking shockwave. Follow up with a strong attack to swing the armament in a sweeping strike."\nUsable on greataxes, warhammers, and colossal weapons.\n\nAsh of War: Earthshaker Location in Elden Ring\nWhere to find Ash of War: Earthshaker:\n\nFrom the Altus Highway Junction Grace. Go due South until you can clearly see the road. As you face South, there should be a square pattern in the road. Ahead or to your left there will be a very small dust cloud in the road. This is the scarab. It does not make noise or leave glowing prints on the ground like others.\nEarthshaker can be obtained killing a slow-moving invisible scarab just South of Altus Highway Junction (found near a statue at the first intersection, you can hear it and see a little cloud of dust moving, note: not a scarab that leaves a light trail).\nThe Scarab is present at any time, but easier to see at night. Using a skill/spell like Glintblade Phalanx may help, as it can target and kill the scarab automatically, if you get close enough.\nSee it on the Elden Ring Map.\n\nAsh of War: Earthshaker Elden Ring Guide\n\nGrants the skill Earthshaker.\nCan be used with any affinity Upgrade.\nCan be applied to the following weapon categories, so long as the weapon allows skill replacement: Colossal Swords, QUESTION
Where can Ash of War: Earthshaker be found in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
From the Altus Highway Junction Grace, go south until you see a square pattern in the road, then look for a slow-moving invisible scarab just south of Altus Highway Junction, easier to spot at night.
