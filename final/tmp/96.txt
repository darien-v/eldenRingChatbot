\n\n Writheblood Ruins \n\nA set of ruins overlooking a forest where the Altus Plateau's Minor Erdtree can be found.\n\nWritheblood Ruins  is a Location in Elden Ring. The Writheblood Ruins  is found in Altus Plateau. Two sequential jumping platforms are found just north of Mirage Rise, take these and head briefly eastward to arrive at Writheblood Ruins; or follow the path east from the Bridge of Iniquity site of grace.\n\nWritheblood Ruins Map\n[Elden Ring Map here]\n\nAll NPCs and Merchants in Writheblood Ruins\n\nNo NPC or Merchant in the area.\n\nAll Items in Elden Ring's Writheblood Ruins\n\nGather & Farm Items\n\nSacramental Bud\nRowa Fruit\n\nUpgrade Materials\n\n??\n\nEquipment and Magic\n\nBloody Helice\n\nUnique, Ashes and Keys\n\n??\n\nElden Ring Writheblood Ruins Creatures, Enemies, and Bosses\n\nRegular Creatures and Enemies\n\nGiant Skeletal Slime\nMagnus the Beast Claw\nRotten Stray\nSkeletal Slime\n\nField Bosses and Bosses\n\nSanguine Noble\n\nWritheblood Ruins Walkthrough\nWritheblood Ruins can be found just north of Sainted Hero's Grave, when you arrive at the location, there will be a couple of Skeletal Slimes in the area, you can completely skip past them and just head down the underground chamber and traverse the mist to fight Sanguine Noble, the boss in the area. Once you traverse the mist, the boss fight will begin. It is recommended that you equip a lantern to get a bit of light, since the area is a bit dark, and it will help you see the blood puddles that the Noble will put on the ground. Every single attack the boss does will stack bleed, so you'll have to watch your meter everytime you get hit. The Noble's attacks are very fast, it has both melee and long-range capabilities. It can throw out its weapon as a projectile, leave blood puddles on the ground, and when it gets in close it will start a flurry of stabs and slashes. When you see his left hand with the dagger up, do not attack with melee, you will get parried, and when you're still a bit low level, it could kill you instantly. Instead, you can use ranged attacks such as sorceries and bows, that cannot be parried. After defeating the boss, you can then open the chest within the room and receive the Bloody Helice.\nIn the northeastern part of the ruins, Magnus the Beast Claw's invasion sign can be found. Why is it recommended to equip a lantern when traversing through the Writheblood Ruins in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
