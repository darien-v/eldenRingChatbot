CONTEXT
\n\n Enchanted Shot \n\n        Archery skill that enlivens the arrow with spiritual essence. The resulting shot will fly faster than regular shots and change its trajectory to follow the target. \n\nEnchanted Shot is a Skill in Elden Ring. Enchanted Shot is a regular skill that can be found in Ashes of War and can be applied to Light Bows and Longbows. Updated to Patch 1.07\n\nHow to get Enchanted Shot in Elden Ring\n\nThe Ash of War: Enchanted Shot does not grant any affinity to an armament, but imparts the Enchanted Shot skill where a spiritual essence is gathered within the arrow, allowing it to fly faster than a regular shot arrow. It also changes its trajectory to follow the target.\nAsh of War: Enchanted Shot - Dropped by a Teardrop Beetle hiding behind a rock near a brazier in Nokron, Eternal City. Elden Ring Map Link.\n\nElden Ring Enchanted Shot Guide, Notes & Tips\n\nThis is an Ash of War Skill, associated with the Ash of War: Enchanted Shot.\nFP Cost: 8 per use.\nThis Skill is not Chargeable\nUpon activation, the user draws the bow further than normal, before releasing a shot that seeks the enemy. This produces a distinctive sound and a small blue aura around the bow. \n\nThe homing property only works when locking on enemies. Works better at longer distances.\nThe arrow will curve and turn if necessary to hit the enemy.\nIf this skill is used without locking-on and without manually aiming, the arrows tend to travel directly to the ground in front.\n\nAlthough the same animation is used for the skill Mighty Shot, this skill does not penetrate through shields.\nThe arrow used with this skill deals 50% more damage than a regular shot and consumes 50% more stamina.\n\nNOTE: The arrow now travels faster, as of Patch 1.07. Previously the projectile speed remained unchanged.\n\nThanks to the faster projectile speed, this weapon skill now allows arrows to reach farther.\n\nIf used without FP: \n\nLoses its tracking properties.\nDoes not receive increased arrow speed.\nLoses distinctive sound and the small blue aura.\nReceives a damage penalty (only deals 5% more damage than a normal shot)\n\nEnchanted Shot QUESTION
Why is Enchanted Shot only effective when locking on to enemies in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
Enchanted Shot is only effective when locking on to enemies in Elden Ring because the homing property of the skill works best at longer distances, causing the arrow to curve and turn if necessary to hit the enemy.
