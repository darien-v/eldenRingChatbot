\n\n Rileigh the Idle \n\nLocation\nAltus Plateau\n\nDrops\n1828  RunesRune ArcFurlcalling Finger RemedyCrepus's VialBlack-Key Bolt x20\n\nRileigh the Idle is an Enemy in Elden Ring. They are one of the assassination targets assigned to you by the Volcano Manor. You can find the summon sign to invade their world in Altus Plateau. Other Invadable NPCs can be found here.\n\n There was a time when Tarnished who had strayed from guidance feared nothing more than utter silence.\n\nElden Ring Rileigh the Idle Locations\nWhere to Find Rileigh the Idle:\n\nYou can invade Rileigh the Idle at Altus Plateau. [Elden Ring Map Link]\n\nElden Ring Rileigh the Idle Drops\n\n1828  Runes\nRune Arc\nFurlcalling Finger Remedy\nCrepus's Vial\nBlack-Key Bolt x20\n\nRileigh the Idle Notes & Tips\n\nWields Crepus's Black-Key Crossbow and Scorpion's Stinger, which applies Scarlet Rot.\nWears the Confessor Set, this armor is very light and thus he is weak to strong attacks that break poise.\nCan heal once using a Crimson Flask.\nConsidering the use of the Scorpion's Stinger, as well as the fact that the Black-Key Bolts they use inflict Scarlet Rot, it is possible that Rileigh is related to Maleigh Marais, somehow. If so, it is also possible that Rileigh actually is Crepus, and used the moniker to distance themself from their family's negative history. This would explain why all Black-Key Bolts inflict Scarlet Rot, and not just those used by Rileigh, as "Crepus" could have created the Black-Key Bolts themself and distributed them to their subordinates. This would also explain why so few Black-Key Bolts are found in the game, as they would all have been made by one person, instead of manufactured on a large scale (which would require sharing
