CONTEXT
\n\n Wild Strikes \n\n        Swing armament with wild abandon. Hold to continue swinging. Can be followed up with a normal or strong attack. \n\nWild Strikes is a Skill in Elden Ring. Wild Strikes is a damaging skill which temporarily allows wild swinging, affecting foes within range. Updated to Patch 1.07.\n\nHow to get Wild Strikes\n\nDefault skill on the Battle Axe\nDefault skill on the Jawbone Axe\nDefault skill for the Ripple Blade\nDropped from a Teardrop Scarab located in the middle of the path leading from the Stormhill Shack site of grace to the Castleward Tunnel site of grace. Elden Ring Map Link.\n\nElden Ring Wild Strikes Guide, Notes & Tips\n\nThis is an Ash of War Skill, associated with the Ash of War: Wild Strikes.\nFP Cost: 2 (10 15)\nThis Skill is not Chargeable\nThis Skill can be Parried\nUpon activation, the user swings around their armament (weapon) to the front with wide overhead strikes. \n\nThis consumes the stated 2 FP.\nDeals 5-6.5 stance dmg per swing depending on weapon type\nThe player must hold the skill button to keep swinging. Maintaining this keeps consuming FP.\nStamina consumption depends of the armament, but remains low overall.\nThis skill resets most of the Poise the player has when used, allowing them to refresh it quickly.\nThe swings do not receive status buildup penalty when hitting an enemy. Can be used for quick buildup on opening windows.\n\nPressing Light Attack during the swinging animation will cause the user to transition into a wide sweeping strike to the front. \n\nThis can be aimed in any direction\nThis consumes the stated 10 FP.\nThe final blow deals 20-28 Stance damage\n\nPressing Heavy Attack during the swinging animation will cause the user to transition into a heavy overhead blow \n\nThis consumes the stated 15 FP.\nThe final blow deals 30-42 Stance damage\n\nThis skill is particularly useful on weapons with a innate status buildup like the Morning Star. \n\nBecause the skill carries no penalties on buildup when used, the player can opt to use greases to their weapons to apply things like Rot, Freeze, Poison and Bleed.\n\nAgainst enemies with long stance-break animations (like Dragons) it can often be more effective to spam Wild Strikes instead of going for the critical hit, provided they have FP remaining.\n\nThis becomes specially true when applying status effects to the enemies.\n\nStance damage for each part of the Skill is as follows:\n\nSwings\nLight Finisher\nHeavy Finisher\n\nCurved Swords, Axes\n5\n20\n30\n\nGreatswords, Curved Greatswords, Greataxes\n5\n22\n30\n\nFlails\n6\n24\n36\n\nHammers\n6.5\n26\n39\n\nGreat Hammers\n6.3\n28\n42\n\nIf used without FP: \n\nLoses most of its damage on the swings.\n\nNOTE: This Skill was changed with Patch 1.07 as follows: \n\nTime between various actions and activation of skills have been shortened.\nAttack Power has been slightly increased.\n\nWild Strikes Demonstration\n\nElden QUESTION
Why is Wild Strikes particularly useful on weapons with innate status buildup like the Morning Star?

ANSWER
Wild Strikes is particularly useful on weapons with innate status buildup like the Morning Star because it carries no penalties on status buildup when used, allowing players to apply things like Rot, Freeze, Poison, and Bleed efficiently.
