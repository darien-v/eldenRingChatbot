\n\n The Four Belfries \n\nAs its name suggests, The Four Belfries is a hill with four large bell towers overlooking Liurnia from the west. Each belfry hosts a waygate that transports travellers to various locations.\n\nThe Four Belfries is a Location in Elden Ring. The Four Belfries is found in Liurnia of the Lakes. Players can reach this location by following the main road along the western landmass heading north and then veering off to the east when the road forks at the Foot of the Four Belfries site of grace.\n\nThe Four Belfries Map\nThe Four Belfries is found near the midpoint of the western landmass in Liurnia. Each waygate is located at the foot of a belfry and requires an Imbued Sword Key to activate.\n\nThe First Belfry: The first waygate is located closest to the foot of the hill and is denoted by the message reading "Crumbling Lands". This transports you to a small sub-area of the Crumbling Farum Azula containing the Pearldrake Talisman. You cannot access the rest of the region from this sub-area.  [Map Link]\nThe Second Belfry: The second waygate is located further up the hill to the east and is denoted by the message reading "Precipice of Anticipation". This transports you to the Chapel of Anticipation, the starting area of the game, to fight the Grafted Scion boss who drops Golden Beast Crest Shield and Ornamental Straight Sword, plus lootables: The Stormhawk King and the Stormhawk Deenh. Note: you are able to soak Lord of Blood's Favor in the blood of a deceased maiden near the starting area of the game, providing an alternative to going to the Church of Inhibition or killing Irina. [Map Link].\nThe Third Belfry: The third waygate is located further up the hill to the west and and is denoted by the message reading "Night Sky Unceasing". This transports you to an otherwise inaccessible location in Nokron, Eternal City, housing the Mottled Necklace and a Lesser Crucible Knight. [Map Link]. The Knight does not drop any loot except for runes.\nThe Fourth Belfry: The final belfry is located at the peak of the hill. This belfry does not contain a waygate, but instead houses a chest containing an Imbued Sword Key. This can be used to unlock a waygate at the First, Second or Third Belfry.\n\nElden Ring The Four Belfries Gallery and Notes\n[other images go here]\n\nAll Elden Ring Locations\n\nAbandoned QUESTION
How can players access the waygates at The Four Belfries in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
Players can access the waygates at The Four Belfries in Elden Ring by obtaining an Imbued Sword Key and using it at each waygate located at the foot of the four belfries.
