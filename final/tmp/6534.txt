CONTEXT
\n\n Blood Blade \n\n        Wound self to coat the armament with blood, then unleash an airborne blood blade that causes hemorrhaging. Can be fired in rapid succession. \n\nBlood Blade is a Skill in Elden Ring. Its Ash of War can be applied to small and medium swords. Updated to Patch 1.07.\n\nHow to get Blood Blade in Elden Ring\n\nDropped by a Teardrop Scarab in Altus Plateau, northeast of Erdtree-Gazing Hill, above a pond. [Map link]\nA special version of this Skill, the Reduvia Blood Blade, can be acquired by equipping the Reduvia dagger.\n\nElden Ring Blood Blade Guide, Notes & Tips\n\nThis is an Ash of War Skill, associated with the Ash of War: Blood Blade.\nFP Cost: 3\nDeals 9 stance damage per slash\nThis Skill is not Chargeable, but can be used repeatedly without additional windup.\nThis Skill cannot be Parried (the Blood projectile can be Parried with Carian Retaliation) \n\nThis Skill can be Parried if performed with no Focus\n\nDamage type is Physical Damage.\nDamage scales with weapon upgrade level and Arcane.\nThis Skill sends streams of blood flying ahead of the caster and can be cast in quick succession for a barrage of projectiles.\nThis Skill costs 45 HP to coat the blade with the user's blood once, at the beginning of each combo.\nIf you hit with the weapon during the animation, it will deal weapon damage and apply status effects, if any.\nThe projectile has a base of 60 bleed build up and scales with arcane.\n\nNOTE: This Skill was changed with Patch 1.07 as follows:\n\nPower has been increased.\nDamage detection to the weapon part has been added.\nDelay between the end of various actions (like using items or attack animations) and being able to perform the skill has been reduced.\n\nBlood Blade Demonstration\n\nElden Ring Skills\n\nAncient Lightning Spear  ♦  Angel's
