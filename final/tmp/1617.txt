Go for backstabs if you can, or just land attacks opportunistically while he's recovering. His moves are slow enough that guard countering is particularly effective. \nMagic and Ranged Users\nRanged players should avoid standing at a medium-long range that will cause him to use his Axe Chain Swing attack, since that's particularly hard to evade. Either use short-range spells from a medium-close range, or keep far away and hit him while he jogs slowly towards you.\n\nFrenzied Duelist Attacks & Counters\n\nAttack\nDescription\nCounter\n\nAxe Swing\nSwings his axe in various ways\nDepending on where he is in his combo, either dodge away and prepare to dodge the rest of the combo or dodge behind him and land an attack or a backstab. Note that only some axe swings are parriable, and it's not obvious which; see the combo chart below for details\n\nBall & Chain\nSwings the ball and chain on the hilt of his axe\nRoll away and wait out the combo\n\nAxe Shove\nShoves the head of his axe forward\nRoll away and wait out the combo\n\nStomp\nLifts its leg briefly and stomps on the ground. Usually does this when the player is behind his back\nRoll left or right\n\nGrab\nRaises his right hand and crouches to the ground before lunging forward into a grab that deal a lot of damage if it connects\nIf you have time, you can just walk behind him and land a few attacks while he's grabbing air. If you're caught by surprise, roll to the left or right\n\nAxe Chain Swing\nTwirls its axe around his head in two wide arcs\nRoll to your left once for each swing. This is only parriable up close just before the first swing\n\nBall & Chain Swing\nSwings the ball on the hilt of his axe four times at progressively longer range\nThe first swing can't hurt you, but the rest can. The chain only deals damage in front of the duelist, so try to dodge behind it\n\nAxe Charge\nPuts the front of its axe on the ground and charges forward before lifting it off the ground\nRoll to the left or right to get out of the way of its charge and prepare to dodge its follow-up swing\n\nRunning Attack\nRuns forward with its axe raised, then spins around swinging twice\nRoll twice, then get in a counterattack while he recovers\n\nBraggart's Roar\nLets loose a furious roar, increasing his attack power and damage negation\nThis attack doesn't actually deal damage, so it's a chance to get QUESTION
What are the best strategies for defeating the Frenzied Duelist boss in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
Use Gavel of Haima for stun-locking and backstab him during slow recovery, circle around for melee users, avoid medium-long range for ranged attacks, counter specific attacks like Axe Swing and Stomp, and dodge other attacks like Ball & Chain or Axe Charge for an effective strategy against Frenzied Duelist in Elden Ring.
