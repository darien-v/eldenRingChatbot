These imposing knights ride atop dark steeds and appear only at night, wielding large flails and halberds. There are several Night's Cavalry that roam the Lands Between. These are optional bosses, as players don't need to defeat them in order to advance in Elden Ring.\n\n The Night's Cavalry, who now wander the dim roads at night, were once led by the Fell Omen and were deliverers of death for great warriors, knights, and champions.\n\nElden Ring Night's Cavalry Location\nNight's Cavalry can be found in:\n\nLimgrave  [More Info] [Map Link]\nWeeping Peninsula [More Info] [Map Link]\nLiurnia South [More Info] [Map Link]\nLiurnia North [More Info] [Map Link]\nCaelid [More Info] [Map Link]\n Altus Plateau [More Info] [Map Link]\nForbidden Lands [More Info] [Map Link]\nDragonbarrow [More Info] [Map Link]\nConsecrated Snowfield [More Info] [Map Link]\n\nNight's Cavalry Combat information\n\nStance: 80\n\nThere is a poise-damage reduction multiplier of 0.2, so every hit deals only 20% of basic poise damage. \nResummoning the horse resets their poise\nPoise cannot be broken while mounted.\n\nIs vulnerable to a  critical hit  after being stance broken or knocked out of horse\nThe horse and rider have separate health bars, and only the rider needs to be killed to end the fight. Killing the horse causes the rider to fall to the ground and become vulnerable to a dismount critical. After some time, the rider will resummon the horse with its Remount attack. It's much more likely to remount quickly if you move far away from it, so stay close to maximize the amount of time it's in a weakened state. \n\nHorse is slightly less resistant to damage than rider.\n\nNegations (or Absorptions)\n\nStandard: 35\nSlash: 35\nStrike: 35\nPierce: 10\n\nMagic: 40\nFire: 40\nLightning: 20\nHoly: 40\n\nThe negation numbers are the % of your damage that gets blocked. For example, if a negation is 60, 40% of that damage by that type will go through and 60% will be negated. Bigger number = less damage. A negation of 100 means no damage goes through, and a negation of -100 mean the enemy takes 2x damage from that source. 0 means damage goes pretty much as is.\n\nElden Ring Night's Cavalry Boss Guide\n\nNight's Cavalry Boss Video Guide\n\nNight's Cavalry Fight Strategy\nThe Best Tips for Night's Cavalry:\n\nUse Lightning Spear to whittle down its health from afar\nUse Glintstone Arc to hit both the horse and its rider at once\nUse a weapon with long reach that deals Pierce Damage, such as a Spear or Halberd\nKnights met at Dragonbarrow and Forbidden Lands can be - with precise maneuvering - tricked into falling from the cliff.\n\nMelee Users\nFighting on foot is recommended. They're fast enough to match your pace on Torrent, and the extra maneuverability and ability to dodge on foot will help a lot. Dismounting is especially important once you kill their horse, as they're much more likely to use Remount if you're further away from them.\nKeep it Close: The rider is surprisingly maneuverable at a distance, so keep the fight close to them, dodging into their attacks and landing hits as you come out of your rolls. They have few attacks that can hit you if you're on their left flank and none that can hit you by the hindquarters, so try to stay there as much as possible. Your safest opportunity for damage is after a Weapon Slam, but you can get in a quick hit while the horse is rearing up as well. Beware of their surprising Leap Attack, which can cover a lot of distance very quickly.\nBait a Charge: Stand a medium distance away to bait the Night's Cavalry into charging you, then dodge in the direction it's moving (ideally aiming for its left flank to limit its available attacks) and attack once or twice. Once you've landed a few hits, get some distance and repeat the process.\nMagic and Ranged Users\nBait a Charge: Stand a medium distance away to bait the Night's Cavalry into charging you, then dodge in the opposite direction from the way it's moving to get some room to cast or use a bow. If you're finding your shots going astray, try firing during the charge when the horse leans its head down and you have a clear shot on the rider.\nHorseback: Time your dashes to evade the Night's Cavalry's attacks and shoot while it's recovering or starting the next attack. Be aware that it can cover a lot of distance very quickly, so focus more on circling it and avoiding its attacks then on getting a large amount of distance.\n\nNight's Cavalry Attacks & Attack can be parried (glaive only)\nNight's Cavalry Lore, Notes & Other Trivia\n\nThey were originally planned to be a wandering encounter, found at random areas around the map, before being put at their set locations. This idea was also planned for the Black Knights of Dark Souls and the Pursuer from Dark Souls II.\nThere are 9 locations where members of the Night's Cavalry can be found, a clear reference to Ring Wraiths from the Lord of the Rings. In the files, they are even called Nazgul.\nIf the player manages to kill the horse with fire damage while hitting the rider as well, the knight will die instantly, no matter how much health it has.\nKilling the rider during the animation related to getting back on their horse or jumping may cause horse to survive and become invincible. The horse will not be hostile to the player if this happens.\nWhile raw number of their poise is not the highest, their effective poise - due to multiplier - is 400. What makes them the hardest enemy to poise-break amongst bosses.\nThe Night's Cavalry is led by Margit, the Fell Omen, as alluded to my Margit's quote if defeated at Stormveil and the description of their Armor Set.\nWhen dismounted, Night's Cavalry seem to have a similar moveset to Halberd Banished Knights excluding the storm attacks and a few other moves. This can look odd on the flail-wielding Night's Cavalry.\n\nNight's Cavalry Combo Chart\n\n Night's Cavalry  Limgrave  \n\nDrops\n2,400  Runes (NG)600  Runes (NG, Co-op)Ash of War: Repeating Thrust\n\nHP\n1,665\n\nElden Ring Night's Cavalry Boss (Limgrave)\n\nThis is an optional boss\nClosest Site of Grace: Agheel Lake North\nMultiplayer is allowed for this boss\nYou cannot summon  Spirit Ashes  for this boss\n\nNight's Cavalry Location\nTo the southeast of the Agheel Lake North Site of Grace, on the bridge over the river. QUESTION
Why do the Night's Cavalry only appear at night in Elden Ring, and what significance does this have for players trying to defeat them?

ANSWER
Night's Cavalry only appear at night in Elden Ring due to their lore as once being deliverers of death for great warriors, knights, and champions; this nighttime setting adds a challenging element for players trying to defeat them, enhancing the atmosphere of the encounters.
