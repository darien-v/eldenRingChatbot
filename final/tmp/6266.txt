CONTEXT
\n\n Gravitas \n\n        Skill originating from the Alabaster Lords, who had skin of stone. Thrust the armament into the ground to create a gravity well. In addition to dealing damage, this attack pulls enemies in. \n\nGravitas is a Skill in Elden Ring. Gravitas is the default skill for the Meteoric Ore Blade and it can also be found in Ashes of War and applied to compatible weapons. Updated to Patch 1.07\n\nHow to get Gravitas in Elden Ring\n\nLooted from a Lesser Alabaster Lord that uses the Gravitas skill as one of its attacks when it fights. You can find the enemy roaming by the beach side at the stone arch that has a bonfire near the Seaside Ruins Site of Grace in south of Limgrave. From the site of grace, simply jump all the way to the beachside using your mount and defeat the enemy. Cost: 13\nDeals 26 stance damage\nThis Skill is not Chargeable\nWave part of this attack cannot be parried \n\nSword part of this skill actually can be parried\n\nUsing this skill casts a purple field around you that compresses on itself and pulls enemies within, dealing damage. \nLow damage but large area of effect.\nAs a gravity type attack, it will bring flying enemies to the ground when used - quite effective against the Sword-wielding birds of Stormveil Castle\nIts large AOE can also be useful for farming crafting items from birds or sheep.  They typically die in one hit and you can often hit many of them with a single use.\nNOTE: This Skill was changed with Patch 1.07 as follows: Poise during casting has been increased.\n\nGravitas Demonstration\n\nElden Ring Skills\n\nAncient QUESTION
How does Gravitas work in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
Gravitas in Elden Ring pulls enemies in with a gravity well, dealing damage, has low damage but large area of effect, can bring flying enemies to the ground, and cannot be parried, with a purple field that compresses around you.
