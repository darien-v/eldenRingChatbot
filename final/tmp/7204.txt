\n\n Clayman \n\nLocation\nSiofra River, Ainsel River\n\nDrops\n 117 - 553 RunesClayman's HarpoonSilver Firefly\n\nClayman is an Enemy in Elden Ring. Claymen are slow-moving humanoids found in large groups that can swarm around unprepared players. These enemies wield different types of weapons - some wield spears with glowing tips, some wield glowing daggers, and others don't wield a weapon and instead uses magic against players. They are mainly found inside ruins and in caves in underground regions.\nThe spirit of two (2) Claymen can be summoned for aid in battle by using the Clayman Ashes.\n\nThe warped remains of priests who searched for revelation in service of the ancient dynasty, they employ two sorceries that produce smaller and larger bubbles.\n\nElden Ring Clayman Locations\nWhere to Find Claymen:\n\nUhl Palace Ruins\nSiofra River\nAinsel River\n\nElden Ring Clayman Drops\n\n 117 - 553 Runes\nClayman's Harpoon (dropped only by those that wield it) (2.00% base)\nSilver Firefly (4.00% base)\n\nClayman Notes & Tips\n\nThey seem to be weak to Strike, and Holy damage. \n\nNot much of a difference between standard and strike damage.\n\nThey resist fire and lightning damage.\nThey are almost always found in large groups, so AOE spells are recommended.\nEasy to backstab due to their slow speed.\nTheir are three variants: the Claymen that use a Clayman's Harpoon, the Claymen that use a knife and throw bolas (both unobtainable), and the unarmed Claymen who cast Bubble sorceries to attack the player.\nThey can roll continuously towards the player.\nUses Oracle Bubbles and Great Oracular QUESTION
How can players effectively deal with Claymen in large groups in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
Players can effectively deal with Claymen in large groups in Elden Ring by using AOE spells, exploiting their weakness to Strike and Holy damage, and taking advantage of their slow speed for easy backstabs.
