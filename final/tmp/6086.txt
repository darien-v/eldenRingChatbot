\n\n Poison Moth Flight \n\n        Slash with a poison-infused blade. If the \n       follow-up strike lands on a poisoned foe, it will deal significant \n       damage. \n\nPoison Moth Flight is a Skill in Elden Ring. Poison Moth Flight is a regular skill that can be found in Ashes of War, it can be used on small and medium Swords (Twinblades excepted). Updated to Patch 1.07.\n\nHow to get Poison Moth Flight in Elden Ring\n\nDropped by Night's Cavalry on Caelid. Elden Ring Map Link.\n\nElden Ring Poison Moth Flight Guide, Notes & Tips\n\nThis is an Ash of War Skill, associated with the Ash of War: Poison Moth Flight.\nFP Cost: 7\nDeals 9 stance damage\nThis Skill is not Chargeable\nThis Skill can be Parried\nUpon activation, the user briefly charges the blade with poison, making a vertical slash. \n\nThis attack deals 250 poison buildup.\nThis attack deals as much damage as a heavy attack.\n\nIf the enemy is poisoned, using this Weapon Skill and hitting said enemy with it will remove the poison, apply accumulative resistance as normal, then deal 250 + 5% of max HP extra damage on hit.\nNote: This Skill was changed with Patch 1.07 as follows: \n\nPoison status buildup and its power against poisoned enemies have been increased from 160 to 250.\nDuration and damage of poison have been increased.\nDelay between the end of various actions (like using items or attack animations) and being able to perform the skill has been reduced.\n\nPoison Moth Flight Demonstration\n\nSerpent Samurai Written Guide\n\nElden Ring Skills\n\nAncient Lightning Spear  ♦  Angel's Wings  ♦  Assassin's Gambit  ♦  Barbaric
