CONTEXT
\nBuffs and Debuffs in Elden Ring are categories of Status Effects. This page covers how Buffs and Debuffs stack, the potency of their effects, their FP costs, and their stat requirements.\nThe tables provided below do not provide an exhaustive list of every status effect in the game, only those that fall into exclusive categories and are prone to being overridden.\n\nSee Status Effects for all status buildups and other effects applied to your character.\nSee Damage Types to identify the best sources and resistances for all damage types.\nSee Special Weaknesses for details on types of enemies weak to particular equipment.\n\nAs of 4/24/2023, this page is up-to-date with Patch 1.09.1.\n\nStacking Buffs & Debuffs in Elden Ring\nPersistent Buffs, their effects denoted by square borders, can be freely stacked:\n\nWeapons with Special Effects\nArmor with Damage) and Exalted Flesh (+20% Physical Damage) would result in +44% Physical Damage).\nFor Skills such as Golden Vow, the Caestus and the Dagger are immediately usable by all Classes, and have the lowest weight among all weapons which can use the Ash of War.\n\nBuffs\nDebuffs\n\nTemporary Buff Categories\n\nWeapon \n\nShield \n\nBody \n\nAura \n\nHealth Regen \n\nStamina Regen \n\nFall Damage QUESTION
How can different buffs and debuffs be strategically stacked for maximum effect in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
Buffs can be stacked multiplicatively in Elden Ring, such as combining Red-Feathered Branchsword (+20% Damage) with Exalted Flesh (+20% Physical Damage) resulting in +44% Physical Damage. Skills like Golden Vow, the Caestus, and the Dagger can be beneficial, offering immediate usability and low weight. Armament buffs need to be wielded in a specific hand and are lost upon swapping, while spells require the right catalyst and may be extended by the Old Lord's Talisman. Different categories of buffs and debuffs can be strategically combined, providing various advantages in combat, like increased damage output, resistance, and other beneficial effects in Elden Ring.
