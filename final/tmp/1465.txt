This giant flower releases clouds of poisonous pollen into the air. This is an optional boss, as players don't need to defeat it in order to advance.\nThe Giant Miranda Sprout is the normal enemy form of this boss. Another Miranda boss appears in the Omenkiller and Miranda Flower duo fight.\n\nMiranda, maiden of the Flower Crucible is said to have been the very first of this breed.\n\nElden Ring Miranda the Blighted Bloom Boss\n\nFound in Tombsward Cave [Map Link]\nThis is an optional boss\nClosest Site of Grace: Tombsward Catacombs\nMultiplayer is allowed for this boss\nYou can summon  Spirit Ashes  for this boss\nThis fight features several Miranda Sprouts as well, but only the Blighted Bloom needs to be defeated to win\n\nMiranda the Blighted Bloom Combat information\n\nHealth: 2,483 HP \nDefense: 102.6\nStance: 100\nParryable: No\nIs vulnerable to a  critical hit  after being stance broken\nDamage: Standard, Strike, Holy\nInflicts: Poison\nDrops 2,100\n\nNegations (or Absorptions)\n\nStandard: 0\nSlash: -40\nStrike: 35\nPierce: 0\n\nMagic: 0\nFire: -40\nLightning: 40\nHoly: 40\n\nThe negation numbers are the % of your damage that gets blocked. Alternate hits with a Torch to interrupt its attacks and a normal melee weapon to deal damage while it's recovering. If you deal damage quickly enough, you can largely ignore the Miranda Sprouts.\nMagic and Ranged Users\nUse spells that prioritize damage over casting speed: it's hard to avoid being poisoned, but the flower's attacks are mostly slow and unable to interrupt you. Inflict Fire Damage if possible—not only is the Miranda quite weak to it, it can interrupt its attacks. Just be sure to get out of the way when its Rain of Light attack starts charging up.\n\nMiranda the Blighted Bloom Attacks & Counters\n\nAttack\nDescription\nCounter\n\nPoison Cloud\nThe flower will slowly close its petals, before releasing a lingering cloud of pollen around it, causing rapid Poison build-up.\nHit the Miranda with Fire Damage to interrupt this attack or burn up the pollen. Otherwise, there's no way to escape the cloud in the enclosed arena.\n\nPoisonCloud Burst\nA variation of the Poison Cloud attack, it will close its petals which shake slightly before unleashing a cloud of poisonous pollen in front of it, knocking its targets back and building up Poison.\nRoll away once you see it close its petals, or interrupt it with Fire Damage. This attack still does low amounts of damage if it connects, however.\n\nRain of Light\nThe flower rises high on its stalk before making an odd sound. The area around its intended target will begin glowing white, before being peppered with beams of light from above.\nMove away from the Miranda when you see it start to rise, then move back in when you see the motes. The attack's radius will be far enough away to give you space to keep attacking.\n\nHead Slam\nIf its target is close to it, the flower will lean back and slam down on its foe, dealing mediocre damage.\nInterrupt the attack with Fire Damage or roll to either side.\n\nMiranda the Blighted Bloom Lore, Notes & Other Trivia\n\nName changed to “Miranda Blossom” in an unknown patch.\n\nMiranda the QUESTION
Why is it important to bring Neutralizing Boluses to cure Poison when fighting Miranda the Blighted Bloom in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
To cure the Poison inflicted by Miranda the Blighted Bloom's attacks, Neutralizing Boluses are essential due to the rapid Poison build-up caused by the boss.
