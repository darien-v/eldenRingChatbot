CONTEXT
\nGreataxes are a type of Weapon in Elden Ring. Greataxes can deal large amounts of damage and can easily break guards,  but have slow attacks, leaving users vulnerable. Players will be required high Strength stats to wield Greataxes propery. \nGreataxes are compatible with a range of different Ashes of War, allowing you to mix and match for whatever your needs may be.\nNote: Since Patch 1.06, the attack speed of Greataxes was increased and the time it takes for rolling to become possible after an attack has been reduced. Also, a fix was introduced for a bug causing additional effects from Spells, Weapon Skills and Items not to be applied correctly when dual-wielding Greataxes.\n\nElden Ring Greataxe Range Comparison\n\nWeapon Range Data for Great Axe in Elden Ring. Credits to user Kaj. \n\nAll Elden Ring Greataxe Guide\nThere are only a few Great Axe choices in game. Some of their pros include heavyset attacks and they have the ability to deal great damage upon a successful hit. However, with the few Greataxes available in the game, your options are limited and players have expressed that these Weapons tend to be underpowered in PvP while there are other Weapon options to explore in other classes that can deal great damage without the heavy and slow features of the Greataxe. Since the release of the game, FromSoftware has attempted a few balancing updates to help with the speed of the Great Axe. Great Axes will be ideal for those who find satisfaction in strong heavy hits and can compensate for the loss of speed in Combat.
