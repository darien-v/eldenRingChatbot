CONTEXT
\nFlails are a type of Weapon in Elden Ring. Flails have long reach and can inflict single powerful strikes against hostile characters, but they are rather slow. \nFlails cannot be parried. However, unlike Whips, it can be used to perform critical attacks.\nFlails have a unique sweetspot mechanic, where hitting with the ball of the weapon deals more damage than the stick part of the weapon.\nFlails can be modified by a number of Ashes of War to change their Skills or Damage Type, so play around with them and see what you like as you acquire more.\nThe default Art of War on most flails, Spinning Chain, is not available as a separate Ash of War as of Patch 1.03. This means you cannot change the damage affinity of these flails without overwriting Spinning Chain.\n\nNOTE: As of Patch 1.08, the speed and distance of some attacks has been increased, and the recovery time has been reduced. (Only when dual-wielding)\nNOTE: As of Patch 1.08, The speed of guard counters, attack speed, and two-hand poise attacks has increased. QUESTION
What are the unique characteristics and mechanics of Flails in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
Flails in Elden Ring have long reach, cannot be parried, feature a unique sweetspot mechanic, can be used for critical attacks, and can be modified by Ashes of War to change Skills or Damage Type.
