\n\n Stray \n\nLocation\nAll across the Lands Between\n\nDrops\n\n 52 - 1829 Runes\nRuin Fragment\nThin Beast Bones\nBeast Blood\nSanctuary Stone\nSacramental Bud\n\nStray is a Wildlife Creature in Elden Ring. Strays are hostile and emaciated canines.\nThe spirit of a Rotten Stray can be summoned for aid in battle by using Rotten Stray Ashes.\n\n...Stray dog.\n...Though it is only a small creature, it is as violent as it is nimble. \n\nStray Location in Elden Ring\nWhere to Find Stray:\n\nPlayers can encounter Strays in the caves of Limgrave, such as Limgrave Tunnels.\nRoaming the west shores of Castle Morne, and inside the castle itself.\nCan be found in the upper parts of The Shaded Castle\nUnsightly Catacombs\nOld Altus Tunnel\nCan be found in parts of Caelid, including Caelid Waypoint Ruins.\nCan be encountered in some sections within Leyndell, Royal Capital.\nCan be found in the Swamp of Aeonia in Caelid.\nCan be found in the Consecrated Snowfield in the Mountaintops of the Giants.\nCan be found in the Mohgwyn Palace area.\nCan be found in Crumbling Farum Azula.\n\nStray Drops in Elden Ring\n\n 52 - 1829 Runes\nThin Beast Bones\nBeast Blood\nRuin Fragment (only from some Large Strays)\nSanctuary Stone (only from Braided Strays from Farum Azula)\nSacramental Bud (only those from Writheblood Ruins)\n\nStray Subtypes\n\nEnemy Type\nBuildup Type\nDrops\nNotes and Locations\n\n Stray \n No buildup type. \n\nThin Beast Bones (8.00% base)\nBeast Blood (4.00% base)\nRuin Fragment (Large Strays only) (15.00% base)\n\nCan be found across the Lands Between.\n\n Rotten Stray \n Inflicts Scarlet Rot buildup \n\nThin Beast Bones (8.00% base)\nBeast Blood (4.00% base)\n\nCan be found in Caelid.They are covered in the same mushrooms that make up the landscape of Caelid.\n\n Blistered Stray \n Inflicts Hemorrhage buildup \n\nThin Beast Bones (8.00% base)\nBeast Blood (4.00% base)\nRuin Fragment (Large Blistered Strays only) (15.00% base)\nSacramental Bud (in Writheblood Ruins only) (3.00% base)\n\nTwo are near the Second Church of Marika, many are in and around the Writheblood Ruins, and many are found around and within the large, shallow pool of blood with the Blistered Giant Crows in the Mohgwyn Palace.Like several other enemies that can inflict Hemorrhage buildup, they are covered in large red blisters.\n\n Braided Stray \n No buildup type. \n\nThin Beast Bones (8.00% base)\nBeast Blood (4.00% base)\nRuin Fragment (Large Braided Strays only) (15.00% base)\nSanctuary Stone (3.00% base)\n\nCan only be found in Crumbling Farum Azula.They wear jewelry and have braided hair (unlike other Strays, which lack most hair) that match that of theAzula Beastmen. This seems to imply they are or were pets of the Azula Beastmen, as Strays have no dexterous hands to braid with, though it is possible their relationship could have been more complex than that, due to the Beastmen's canine biology.\n\nElden Ring Stray Notes & Tips\n\nStrays are fast and vicious; using a good shield is a good way to deal with them.\nDeals Standard Damage. Some variants can additionally inflict Blood loss or Scarlet Rot.\nLike most animal-type creatures, they are susceptible to Fire Damage and Status Effects (most notably Hemorrhage).\nThere are occasionally somewhat larger individual Strays that have more health and drop more Runes. They have a slightly different color than the normal-sized Strays.\nStrays are essentially the same as Dogs in that they have the same behavior, moves, and drops, though Dogs do not have buildup subtypes. Strays are also far more common.\nAt one point of Elden Ring post-launch life, Blistered Strays, or as they were called at the time, "Bleed dogs," were the most dangerous creatures in The Lands Between. This was because of a bug related to how game registered their jump attack. QUESTION
Why are Strays found in specific locations across the Lands Between in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
Strays are found in specific locations in Elden Ring such as Limgrave, Castle Morne, Caelid, Leyndell, and more, to provide diverse encounters for players and enhance the exploration aspect of the game world.
