Mausoleum Foot Soldier Set is one of six foot soldier sets, and like the other Mausoleum sets only has three pieces. Sets of Armor in Elden Ring are typically composed of four armor pieces which are Helms, Chest Armor, Gauntlets, and Leg Armor.\n\nWhere to find Mausoleum Foot Soldier Set in Elden Ring\n\nThe Bloodsoaked Tabard drops from Mausoleum Foot Soldiers.\nThey can be found south of Apostate Derelict around the nearby Wandering Mausoleum. They are in groups with Mausoleum Soldiers and Mausoleum Knights and can be identified by the horns they carry.\nThe Foot Soldier Gauntlets and Foot Soldier Greaves can only drop from Godrick Foot Soldiers.\n\nMausoleum Foot Soldier Set Armor Pieces in Elden Ring\n\nBloodsoaked Tabard\nFoot Soldier Gauntlets\nFoot Soldier Greaves\n\nMausoleum Foot Soldier Set Elden Ring Notes and Tips\n\nThis set does not possess a helm, as Mausoleum Foot Soldiers do not have a head.\nAlthough, the rusted and red-stained tabard seems to indicate that the soldier, at one point, did possess a head.\nPoise updated to patch 1.07. QUESTION
Why do Mausoleum Foot Soldiers not have a head, and what does the rusted and red-stained tabard signify about their past?

ANSWER
Mausoleum Foot Soldiers do not have a head because the rusted and red-stained tabard implies they once did possess a head.
