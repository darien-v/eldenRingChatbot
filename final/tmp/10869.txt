Gelmir's Fury spell makes magma come out from the ground covering a large area. Updated to Patch 1.07.\n\nOne of the sorceries developed from the magma of Mt. Gelmir.Conjures a surge of magma from the earth, covering the area.Charging extends the surge of magma.\nThis sorcery is held to represent the fury of the volcano, but the arrogance of attempting to harness it is solely that of men and serpents.\n\nGelmir's Fury Location in Elden Ring\nThe Gelmir's Fury spell can be found in the following location:\n\nGiven by Recusant Bernahl at Volcano Manor after completing his request. [Elden Ring Interactive Map Link]\nIf Recusant Bernahl departs from Volcano Manor before his quest is completed (e.g. because Rykard is killed), Gelmir's Fury can still be obtained by killing Bernahl when invaded in Crumbling Farum Azula. This is no longer applicable as of version: 1.09.1\nVideo Location\n\nElden Ring Gelmir's Fury Guide\n\nMagma Sorcery\nStamina Cost: 38\nDeals fire damage\nAs it deals fire damage, Gelmir's Fury can be used to reset an enemy's frost buildup, which can be useful for quickly proccing Frostbite multiple times. Note that multiple Frostbite procs will only result in more damage, and will not stack Frostbite's reduced damage negation.\nNote: As of Patch 1.07, FP cost has been significantly reduced from 27 to 16, attack power was increased and the casting time and recovery time were both reduced. In addition, the projectiles are less spread out from where you are facing, making it easier to hit enemies in front of you. The first part of the spell has increased damage and ability to stagger enemies.\n\nElden Ring Gelmir's Fury Notes and Tips\n\nUpdated to patch 1.07. QUESTION
What are the effects and properties of Gelmir's Fury spell in Elden Ring after the update to Patch 1.07?

ANSWER
Gelmir's Fury spell now has a significantly reduced FP cost of 16 (3), increased attack power, reduced casting and recovery time, increased damage and ability to stagger enemies, and less spread out projectiles, making it easier to hit enemies in front of you.
