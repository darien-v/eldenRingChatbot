Boars are hostile wild swines even if not attacked. They are a quite reliable source of crafting resources like Lumps of Flesh or Hefty Beast Bones.\n\nThe Boars of Limgrave are less aggressive than normal, but should not be assumed to be passive\n\nBoar Location in Elden Ring\nWhere to Find Boar:\n\nPlayers can encounter Boar in Forests of Limgrave.\nSiofra River\n\nElden Ring Boar Drops and Drop Rates\n\n Item \n Drop Rate \n Additional Info \n\nRunes\n100%\n 4 - 16 Runes\n\nLump of Flesh\n25.00%\nBoth "A" and "B" Variations.\n\nHefty Beast Bone\n15.00%\nBoth "A" and "B" Variations.\n\nOld Fang\n5.00%\nBoth "A" and "B" Variations.\n\nBeast Blood\n5.00%\n"A" Variation only.\n\nGold-Tinged Excrement\n15.00%\nBoth "A" and "B" Variations.\n\nRunes:\n\n4  (Stormhill - Forested West Cliffside)\n4  (Weeping Peninsula - Castle Morne Approach Northwest Cliffside)\n16  (Moonlight Altar - West of Deep Ainsel Well)\n9  (Altus Plateau - Mirage Rise; Mt. For example, if an absorption is 60, 40% of that damage by that type will go through and 60% is absorbed. Bigger number = less damage. An absorption of 100 means no damage goes through, and a resistance of -100 mean the enemy takes 2x damage from that source. 0 means damage goes pretty much as is.\n\nRESISTANCES\n\nPoison: 63\nScarlet Rot: 63\nHemorrhage: 63\nFrostbite: 63\nSleep: 63\n\nThe resistance numbers are the buildup amount to trigger it. Note that these go down over time, and increase each time the effect procs.\n\nElden Ring Boar Notes & Tips\n\nThere are actually two different versions of boars in Elden Ring.  \n\nBoar with long tusks: Aggressive towards the player and will attack on sight. QUESTION
How can players differentiate between the aggressive and passive versions of boars in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
Boar with long tusks is aggressive and attacks, while boar with small tusks runs away from players.
