\n\n Yelough Anix Ruins \n\nA set of ruins found west in the snowfield. Its inhabitants have been afflicted by frenzy.\n\nYelough Anix Ruins is a Location in Elden Ring. The Yelough Anix Ruins is found in the west of the Consecrated Snowfield. Players can reach this location by riding northwest from the Consecrated Snowfield site of grace. The snowfield itself is a hidden location that is accessed by finding the Haligtree Medallion (Left) and Haligtree Medallion (Right) and activating the Grand Lift of Rold.\n\nYelough Anix Ruins Map\nSee the Yelough Anix Ruins' location on the [Elden Ring Map here].\n\nAll NPCs and Merchants in Yelough Anix Ruins\n\nThere are no NPCs and Merchants in this location\n\nAll Items in Elden Ring's Yelough Anix Ruins\n\nGather & Farm Items\n\nEye of Yelough\nGolden Rune (13)\nRimed Rowa\nYellow Ember\n\nUpgrade Materials\n\nThere are no upgrade materials in this location\n\nEquipment and Magic\n\nUnendurable Frenzy\n\nUnique, Ashes and Keys\n\nStonesword Key\n\nElden Ring Yelough Anix Ruins Creatures, Enemies, and Bosses\n\nRegular Creatures and Enemies\n\nGiant Rat\nRat\nTroll\n\nField Bosses and Bosses\n\nThere are no bosses in this location\n\nYelough Anix Ruins Walkthrough\nOf note in these ruins is the Unendurable Frenzy spell found in a chest in the underground treasure room. The entrance to the room is found west of the central section of the ruins, surrounded by walls from all sides. The maddened Giants can destroy the walls using Unendurable Frenzy (the cascading orange lights that emerge from their eyes), allowing you jump the wall using Torrent. Alternatively, the larger structure to the east of the basement has on its western side a sloped broken pillar that can be used to Torrent double-jump. QUESTION
What strategy can be used to access the underground treasure room in Yelough Anix Ruins while avoiding the maddened Giants?

ANSWER
To access the underground treasure room in Yelough Anix Ruins while avoiding the maddened Giants, you can use Torrent to jump over the walls or the broken pillar on the larger structure to double-jump.
