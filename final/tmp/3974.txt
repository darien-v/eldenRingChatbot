\n\n Scarlet Rot Status Effect \n\nEffect\nInflicts damage quickly over a long period\n\nMitigated by\nImmunizing Cured MeatImmunizing Horn Charm\n\nCured by\nFlame, Cleanse MePreserving Boluses\n\nResistive Stat\nImmunity\n\nScarlet Rot is a Status Effect in Elden Ring. Status Effects are various buffs or ailments that apply either a beneficial or detrimental effect to the target. Status Effects can be applied on both enemies and players alike. Status Effects are triggered after a buildup meter is filled by repeated application of attacks that cause the effect. Both players and enemies can have varying resistances to a given Status Effect.\n\nScarlet Rot Information\nScarlet Rot does damage over time based on the target's health. Damage does not scale with weapon. The effect deals damage at a faster rate than poison, but has an overall shorter duration.Immunity will slow the buildup of the effect on players, but will not affect the damage taken from the proc.\n\nScarlet Rot Effects\nTargets afflicted with Scarlet Rot lose HP and take damage.\n\nScarlet Rot on weapons inflicts (0.18% Max HP) + 15 per second for 90 seconds, for a total of 16.2% Max HP + 1350 damage over its full duration.\nScarlet Rot on Rotten Breath, Ekzykes's Decay, and Scarlet Aeonia inflict (0.33% Max HP) + 13 per second for 90 seconds, for a total of 29.7% Max HP + 1170 damage over its full duration.\nMalenia's Scarlet Rot inflicts (3.3% Max HP) + 26 per second for 300 seconds.\nThe Swamp of Aeonia's Scarlet Rot inflicts (2% Max HP) + 8 per second for 180 seconds.\nThe Lake of Rot's Scarlet Rot inflicts (2% Max HP) + 30 per second for 180 seconds.\nImmunity will slow the buildup of the effect on players, but will not affect the damage taken from the proc. Immunity can be increased by adding points to Vigor.\n\nScarlet Rot effect and Players\nThis section lists information pertaining to the Scarlet Rot effect and its relation to Players\n\nScarlet Rot
