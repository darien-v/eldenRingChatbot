\n\n Moonlight Greatsword \n\n        Raise the sword aloft, bathing it in the light of the dark moon. \n       Temporarily increases magic attack power and imbues blade with frost. \n       Heavy attacks release blasts of moonlight. \n\nMoonlight Greatsword is a Skill in Elden Ring. Moonlight Greatsword is a the unique skill found on the Dark Moon Greatsword. Updated to Patch 1.07.\n\nHow to get Moonlight Greatsword\n\nDefault skill for the Dark Moon Greatsword\n\nElden Ring Moonlight Greatsword Guide\n\nThis is a Unique Skill\nFP Cost: 32\nLasts 60 seconds\nAdds 65 Magic Damage and 15 Frost buildup to attacks\nThis skill is a weapon buff, chargeable* weapon skill that deals magic damage using heavy attacks. This means it is affected by a wide variety of buffs and talismans: \n\n*The Skill itself is not technically Chargeable, but the projectiles fired by heavy attacks benefit from charging the heavy attack, so they are affected by the Godfrey Icon, but not by the Axe Talisman, curiously.\nAs part of a skill, the projectiles are affected by the Shard of Alexander or the Warrior Jar Shard.\nThe Axe Talisman and Spiked Cracked Tear still affect the physical swings of the weapon should they connect with an enemy, but not the projectiles.\nThe Magic Scorpion Charm and Terra Magica affect both the swings and the projectiles since they deal magic damage.\nThe Spellblade Set affects the projectiles as well, since they are part of a magic damage dealing weapon skill.\nSince it counts as a weapon buff, it is stackable with unique, shield and body buffs, such as the Magic-Shrouding Cracked Tear, Rallying Standard or Contagious Fury.\nThe projectiles can be parried using Golden Retaliation.\n\nThis Skill's Heavy attack can be Parried\nThe projectiles deal 22 stance damage, or 35 when charged.\n\nThey deal 120% status effect buildup, or 150% when charged. This means it deals 84 frost buildup or 105 when charged.\nCharged projectiles deal roughly 28% more magic damage.\n\nIf the weapon hits while using the skill, it deals full status build up, stance damage and roughly 50% damage, or 65% when charged.\nOnly the skill itself that infuses the blade with magic consumes FP. Launching energy waves using the heavy attack while the skill is active only consumes stamina.\n\nNote: This Skill was changed with Patch 1.07 as follows:\n\nStamina consumption for strong and charged attacks has been reduced. (Makes some of the builds buffed, like Moonlight Crusader)\nDamage detection to the weapon part has been added.\n\nBuilds with Moonlight Greatsword\n\nDarkmoon Spellblade Written Guide\n\nDark Knight Written Guide\n\nElden Ring Skills Demonstration\n\nMoonlight Crusader Written Guide\n\nElden Ring Moonlight Greatsword Notes & Tips\n\nUpdated to patch 1.07. QUESTION
Why is the Moonlight Greatsword considered a unique and valuable skill in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
The Moonlight Greatsword is considered unique and valuable in Elden Ring for its ability to temporarily increase magic attack power, imbue the blade with frost, and release blasts of moonlight during heavy attacks.
