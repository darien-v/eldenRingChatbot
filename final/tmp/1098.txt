Ancient Hero of Zamor is an agile swordwielder that uses frost magic and is found in multiple locations. This is an optional boss, as players don't have to defeat it to advance to the Legacy Dungeon.\nKnights of Zamor are the normal enemy variants of this boss.\n\n These long-lived warriors, clad in biting, freezing winds, are said to have been the mortal enemies of the Fire Giants since time immemorial.\n\nElden Ring Ancient Hero of Zamor Location\n\nWeeping Evergaol [More Info] [Map Link]\nSainted Hero's Grave [More Info] [Map Link]\nGiant-Conquering Hero's Grave [More Info] [Map Link]\n\nAncient Hero of Zamor Combat information\n\nStance: 80\nParryable: Yes\nIs vulnerable to a  critical hit  after being stance broken or parried\nDamage: Slash, Magic\nInflicts: Frostbite\nIn Phase 2, all of its physical attacks do additional Magic damage and inflict Frostbite\n\nNegations (or Absorptions)\n\nStandard: 10\nSlash: 10\nStrike: 10\nPierce: 0\n\nMagic: 0\nFire: -20\nLightning: -20\nHoly: 0\n\nThe negation numbers are the % of your damage that gets blocked. For example, if a negation is 60, 40% of that damage by that type will go through and 60% will be negated. Bigger number = less damage. A negation of 100 means no damage goes through, and a negation of -100 mean the enemy takes 2x damage from that source. 0 means damage goes pretty much as is.\n\nElden Ring Ancient Hero of Zamor Boss Guide\n\nAncient Hero of Zamor Boss Video Guide\n\nAncient Hero of Zamor Fight Strategy\nThe Best Tips for Ancient Hero of Zamor:\n\nGlintblade Phalanx and Glintstone Arc can work wonders as they often avoid projectiles.\nAvoid relying on your shield, since it won't be very effective in Phase 2\n\nMelee Users\nFree Damage: At the beginning of the fight, run in and get in a few hits. They'll often just dodge away once they're fully spawned, although they may also do a Spinning Swing. It's up to you whether the risk is worth the extra damage. You can get in free hits again while it's using Imbue Frost during the change from Phase 1 to Phase 2, which lasts 9 full seconds and doesn't do any damage.\nBait & Strike: Stay outside its swing radius to bait out distance-closing attacks like Leaping Slam and the double clockwise swing combo. In Phase 2, Spring Forward and Triple Swing work as well. These moves tend to have fairly long recovery times, so you can get in a hit or two before backing off again and repeating the process. If you manage your distance well enough, you may also be able to run to the side and get in a hit when it uses Frost Breath as well.\nParrying: Most of this boss's sword swings are parryable (consult the Attacks & Counters chart for exact details), and it tends to have relatively telegraphed wind-ups. If your timing is good, strap on a Buckler and this guy might be a breeze. \n\nMagic and Ranged Users\nStand Back: The Ancient Hero's only fully ranged attack is Frost Breath, which is easy to dodge if you see it coming. This makes it relatively straightforward to stand back and attack from a distance. Just be wary of its distance-closing melee attacks, and keep enough stamina in reserve to dodge away once it tries to close in. It can even be useful to try to stay a bit closer to bait out a slower attack like Spinning Swing or Zamor Ice Storm, and then punish it from afar.\nWatch Your Elements: Faith users and archers should keep in mind this boss's weakness to Fire and Lightning damage. Flame Sling and Firebone Arrows are good early options that can take advantage of this weakness.\n\nAncient Hero of Zamor Attacks & Counters\n\nAttack\nDescription\nCounter\n\nSwing\nSwings its sword in a variety of directions\nParry or dodge away and wait for the combo to end. Blocking isn't recommended, because you'll still take Magic damage and build Frostbite in Phase 2. This is usually parriable, but not if the Hero swings while retreating\n\nSpinning Swing\nCrouches down and spins its sword around a full turn and a half\nRoll away once, then come back and get in a few hits during the very long recovery\n\nLeaping Slam\nJumps in the air and slams its sword down in front of it\nRoll forward when you see the sword become vertical, then get in a hit during the long recovery\n\nFrost Breath\nKneels down and blows a stream icy wind in in front of cone that gradually grows larger as it travels. \nAs soon as you see it kneel, run or jump to the side to avoid being in the area of effect. If you're close enough, you can move to its side or back and get in some free hits while it does this attack\n\nFrost Orb\nPuts its left hand over its right shoulder and quickly creates a sphere of magic around it, knocking enemies back and dealing minor damage. \nRoll away as soon as you see its arm move. Take care not to get hit by this too much, as it gives considerable Frost build-up.\n\nImbue Frost\nOnce it goes below 60% health, it falls to its knees, then enchants its sword with frost magic before rising and twirling around, beginning Phase 2\nRun towards and wail on it as much as you can, as it becomes much more aggressive once it switches phases.\n\nPhase 2\n\nSpring Forward\nKneels briefly, then leaps forward into a counterclockwise swing\nRoll towards it once you see the sword start to fall, then get in a hit during recovery\n\nLeaping Swings\nJumps into the air swinging its sword upwards, then swings it downwards as it falls\nRoll to either side\n\nTwiple Swing\nJumps into the air and swings its sword downwards and clockwise, then swings twice clockwise in quick succession once it lands\nRoll away twice and then attack once it finishes its third swing\n\nIce Burst\nShoves its sword into the ground, then pulls it out creating a small burst of ice\nThere's enough time to get a hit in before it pulls out the sword if you're careful. Otherwise, just keep your distance until the move is over.\n\nZamor Ice Storm\nBuilds up frost magic in its palm, then touches it to the ground to create a large whirlwind of ice that lasts a couple seconds \nAlthough this has a long windup, it's very difficult to stagger it out of the attack so it's best to just leave when you see it charging up. This is a great opportunity for getting in some ranged damage\n\n: Attack can be parried\nAncient Hero of Zamor Lore, Notes & Other Trivia\n\nOther Notes and Trivia Go Here\n\nAncient Hero of Zamor Combo Chart\n\n Ancient Hero of Zamor  Weeping Evergaol  \n\nDrops\n5,400, Radagon's Scarseal\n\nHP\n2,570\n\nElden Ring Ancient Hero of Zamor Boss (Weeping Evergaol)\n\nThis is an optional boss\nClosest Site of Grace: Fourth Church of Marika\nMultiplayer is not allowed for this boss\nYou cannot summon  Spirit Ashes  for this boss\n\nWhere to Find Ancient Hero of Zamor\nThe Ancient Hero of Zamor is held captive in Weeping Evergaol, which is just down the hill to the northeast of the First Church of Marika. What strategies can be employed to defeat the Ancient Hero of Zamor field boss in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
Employ Glintblade Phalanx, avoid shield reliance in Phase 2, bait distance-closing attacks, utilize parrying against sword swings, watch for Frost Breath, consider elemental weaknesses for ranged attacks.
