\n\n Lord of Blood's Exultation \n\nWeight\n0.9\n\nEffect \n\n Raises attack power by 20% (12% in PvP) for 20 seconds when  \n       Blood Loss occurs in the vicinity \n\nLord of Blood's Exultation is a Talisman in Elden Ring. Lord of Blood's Exultation raises attack power by 20% for 20 seconds when Blood Loss occurs in the vicinity. Players can use Talismans in Elden Ring to boost a variety of Stats.\n\nA talisman depicting the exultation of the Lord of Blood.\nRaises attack power when blood loss occurs in the vicinity.\n"Render up you offerings of blood to your Lord. Drench my consort's chamber. Slake his cocoon's thirst. His awakening shall herald the dawn of our dynasty"\n\nLord of Blood's Exultation Effect in Elden Ring\nLord of Blood's Exultation raises attack power by 20% for 20 seconds when Blood Loss occurs in the vicinity. (Effect is reduced to 12% in PvP.)\n\nWhere to find Lord of Blood's Exultation in Elden Ring\n\nDrops from Esgar, Priest of Blood in Leyndell Catacombs. [Map link]\nVideo Location\n\nElden Ring Lord of Blood's Exultation Notes & Tips\n\nSell value:  500\nThe effect stacks with the White Mask.\nThe attack power boost also affects the damage of spells (Sorceries and Incantations).\nIn PvP, Blood Loss that does not deal damage will still increase your attack power.\nThe effect triggers when the wearer suffers Blood Loss. (And using Seppuku will also grant the bonus.)\nThe effect triggers for Mohg as well during any of his boss fights.\nNote: Patch 1.09: Effect reduced from 20% to 12% in PvP.\n\nColossus Guardian QUESTION
How does the effect of Lord of Blood's Exultation talisman in Elden Ring differ between PvE and PvP encounters?

ANSWER
The effect of Lord of Blood's Exultation talisman raises attack power by 20% for 20 seconds when Blood Loss occurs in PvE encounters and 12% in PvP encounters in Elden Ring.
