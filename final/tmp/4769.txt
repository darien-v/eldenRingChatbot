This one is the red flask in your inventory. The blue flask will help with replenishing FP. This flask can be upgraded and will replenish after resting at a Site of Grace. To use this, players will need to have this item equipped where it can be accessed from the D-pad on the controller. The Use Item button is usually the square button on the PlayStation or the X button on the X box. \n\nUpgrading Flasks\n\nFlask Upgrades can be done while resting at a Site of Grace. You should be able to access the Flask option which will bring up more menu options and will allow you to Add charge to flask, Increase amount replesnished by flasks, and allocate flask charges.\nGolden Seeds are required in order to add charges to your flask. Allocating Flask Charges will allow you to adjust how many charges will go in either the red flask or the blue flassk. \n\nFlask Quick Access\n\nThe default way to look for your flask would be to cycle through your inventory items displayed on the bottom slot of your items towards the lower left corner of your screen. It is recommended that you instead equip the flask to your Pouch to be able to access it through quick access. This also allow you to then allocate the flask to the desired slot which will also bind it to a quick access button without having through cycle through the inventory. \n\nCrimson Tears Other Notes\n\nWhether your character is a mage or not, even at low Faith (such as 12 for Bestial Vitality), Cerulean flasks are significantly more efficient at healing HP than Crimson are. However, Crimson flasks are much faster at healing. With this in mind, a Faith dip is worth considering for any build as a way to stretch flask usage.\nAlso known as Red Flask. QUESTION
Why is it important to upgrade the Flask of Crimson Tears in Elden Ring using Golden Seeds and Sacred Tears?

ANSWER
Upgrading the Flask of Crimson Tears in Elden Ring using Golden Seeds and Sacred Tears increases its maximum uses, restorative potency, and efficiency in healing, aiding in survivability during gameplay.
