\n\n Beast's Roar \n\n        Unleash a beastly roar, rending the air as a forward-traveling projectile. \n\nBeast's Roar is a Skill in Elden Ring. Beast's Roar is a regular skill that can be found in Ashes of War and applied to all melee armaments.\n\nHow to get Beast's Roar\n\nBestial Sanctum: Reward from Gurranq, Beast Clergyman after giving him the fourth Deathroot. Elden Ring Map Link.\n\nElden Ring Beast's Roar Guide, Notes & Tips\n\nThis is an Ash of War Skill, associated with the Ash of War: Beast's Roar.\nFP Cost: 10\nDeals 0 stance damage\nThis Skill not Chargeable\nThis Skill cannot be Parried\nDamage is increased by the Shard of Alexander and Roar Medallion talismans, and by wielding a Highland Axe (works when two-handing another weapon, but wielding 2 does not increase the effect).\nFairly high range and large projectile size, but no tracking at all.  \nAs a ranged skill, damage is based on weapon upgrade level and dexterity only.\nDeals very little damage.\nDeals strike type damage.\nUseful on weapons like Claws and Fists as a way to engage enemies that stay out of range of your close range attacks\n\nBeast's Roar QUESTION
What are the benefits and limitations of using Beast's Roar as a ranged skill in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
Benefits include increased damage with specific talismans and weapons like Highland Axe, useful for engaging distant enemies with little damage; limitations are no tracking, low damage output, and based only on weapon upgrade level and dexterity.
