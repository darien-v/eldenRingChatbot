\n\n Giant Dragonfly \n\nLocation\nNear swampy areas in the Lands Betwen\n\nDrops\n\n 4 - 41  RunesGreat Dragonfly Head\n\nGiant Dragonfly is a Wildlife Creature in Elden Ring. These insects are hostile, and are often seen as a nuisance, due to their erratic flight pattern and rather weak attacks.\n\nGiant Dragonfly Location in Elden Ring\nWhere to Find Giant Dragonflies:\n\nPlayers can encounter Great Dragonflies in the humid Agheel Lake regions of Limgrave, near the Dragon-Burnt Ruins. They are also found near small bodies of water.\nGreat Dragonflies can also be found in the Swamp of Aeonia in Caelid. These variants are much larger than most.\n\nElden Ring Giant Dragonfly Combat Information\n\nHealth: 17 HP\nPoise: 0\n\nABSORPTIONS\n\nPhy (Standard): -40\nPhy (Slash): -10\nPhy (Strike): -40\nPhy (Pierce): -40\n\nMagic: -40\nFire: -100\nLightning: -40\nHoly: -40\n\nThe absorption numbers are the % of your damage that gets blocked. For example, if an absorption is 60, 40% of that damage by that type will go through and 60% is absorbed. Bigger number = less damage. An absorption of 100 means no damage goes through, and a resistance of -100 mean the enemy takes 2x damage from that source. 0 means damage goes pretty much as is.\n\nRESISTANCES\n\nPoison: 84 *\nScarlet Rot: 84 *\nHemorrhage: 63\nFrostbite: 20\nSleep: 63\n\n* Giant Dragonfly in West Aeonia Swamp (Caelid) are immune to Poison and Scarlet Rot.\nThe resistance numbers are the buildup amount to trigger it. For example, if a resistance is 100 you must deal 100 points of whatever aux buildup to trigger it. Note that these go down over time, and increase each time the effect procs.\n\nElden Ring Fallen Hawk Soldier Drops and Drop Rates\n\n Item \n Drop Rate \n Additional Info \n\nRunes\n100%\n  4 - 41 \n\nGreat Dragonfly Head\n75.00%\nN/A\n\nRunes: \n\n4 ~ 6  (Limgrave, Weeping Peninsula, Siofra River)\n5  (Liurnia of the Lakes - Revenger's Shack)\n9  (Altus Plateau - Wyndham Ruins, Wyndham Catacombs Entrance)\n7 ~ 10  (Mt. Magic will often miss them. Using a Shortbow can help immensely.\nThey tend to start circling the player once they have been locked onto. Prepare to fire your bow, then quickly lock on and fire to prevent them from moving away.\nThe ones around the Swamp of Aeonia are significantly larger than others.\nThere are two variants, one that is grey and has smaller jaws, and a green variant with larger mandibles\nTheir appearance suggests that they are based on Dobsonflies, an insect that are common around wetlands. QUESTION
Who are the potential locations to find the Giant Dragonflies in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
Great Dragonflies are encountered in the humid Agheel Lake regions of Limgrave, near the Dragon-Burnt Ruins, and also in the Swamp of Aeonia in Caelid.
