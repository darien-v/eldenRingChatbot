This Elden Ring Borealis, the Freezing Fog Guide features boss locations, tips, strategies and videos on how to defeat Borealis easily, as well as boss stats and lore for Borealis. Bosses are unique and challenging Enemies that can be encountered all over the Lands Between. Great Enemy bosses can drop unique Spirit Ashes, Weapons, Spells, and other various Items. This is an optional boss, as players don't need to defeat it in order to advance in Elden Ring. However, doing so yields useful items, as well as Runes.\n\nOther bosses with the same basic attacks are Flying Dragon Agheel, Flying Dragon Greyll, Glintstone Dragon Smarag, Glintstone Dragon Adula, and Decaying Ekzykes. Lesser Dragon and Glintstone Dragon is a normal enemy with the same basic attacks.\n\nThe ice dragons were once lords of the mountaintops long ago, until they were defeated by the Fire Giants and chased from the peak.\n\nElden Ring Borealis the Freezing Fog Boss\n\nThis is an optional boss\nClosest Site of Grace: Spiritcaller Cave\nMultiplayer is allowed for this boss\nYou can summon  Spirit Ashes  for this boss\n\nElden Ring Borealis the Freezing Fog Location\nIn the middle of the blizzard on the frozen lake southeast of the Freezing Lake Site of Grace. QUESTION
Why do players not need to defeat Borealis, the Freezing Fog in order to advance in Elden Ring?

ANSWER
Players do not need to defeat Borealis, the Freezing Fog in order to advance in Elden Ring because it is an optional boss, although doing so yields useful items, as well as Runes.
