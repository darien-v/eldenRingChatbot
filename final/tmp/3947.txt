\n\n Assassin's Gambit \n\n        Skill that masks the user's presence at the cost of a self-inflicted wound. Grants near-invisibility and silences footsteps. \n\nAssassin's Gambit is a Skill in Elden Ring. Assassin's Gambit is a regular skill that can be found in Ashes of War, and this skill can be used on Daggers, Straight Swords, Katanas, Twinblades, Thrusting Swords.\nHow to get Assassin's Gambit in Elden Ring\n\nThis is an Ash of War Skill, associated with the Ash of War: Assassin's Gambit.\nSold by Knight Bernahl in Volcano Manor for 6500 . Note that you will need to fulfill the first contract at Volcano Manor to have access to Knight Bernahl's shop. Elden Ring Map Link.\n\nElden Ring Assassin's Gambit Guide, Notes & Tips\n\nFP Cost: 5 on use.\nHP Cost: 35 on use.\nThis Skill is not Chargeable\nThis Skill cannot be Parried\nWhen activated, the user raises their weapon and brings their left hand to the weapon, quickly slicing it and providing a special buff.\n\nActivating the skill does not produce any sound that the enemies can detect. This allows its use behind an enemy without getting detected.\n\nLasts 30 seconds.\nThe buff grants partial invisibility and silences the user's footsteps in tandem. \n\nPartial invisibility makes enemies harder to detect you at range.\nSilenced footsteps allows the user to move near enemies (even while sprinting) without being detected as long as they are out of their line of sight.\n\nBecause of the combined stealth-related buffs, this skill is a good pick for running quickly through troublesome areas, such as Miquella's Haligtree or Ordina.\nThis buff stays active if you switch weapons.\n\nThis allows you to use one weapon slot to cast this skill cheaply, then immediately switch back to the weapon you actually intend to use.\nThe buff does not seem to be overwritten by any other buffs obtained from spells.\n\nAssassin's Gambit Demonstration\n\nElden Ring Skills\n\nAncient Lightning Spear  ♦  Angel's Wings  ♦  Barbaric Roar  ♦  Barrage  ♦  Barricade Shield  ♦  Bear QUESTION
Why should players consider using the Assassin's Gambit skill in Elden Ring for navigating troublesome areas efficiently?

ANSWER
Players should consider using the Assassin's Gambit skill in Elden Ring for navigating troublesome areas efficiently because it grants partial invisibility and silences footsteps, allowing them to move undetected by enemies and swiftly travel through challenging locations.
